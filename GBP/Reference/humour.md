# Humour Reference — Vaidik's Comic Register

> Used by Claude when writing GBP posts for Num Nums Bakery.
> His humour is subtle. Miss it and you'll write something flat. Overdo it and it won't sound like him.

---

## What his humour actually is

Vaidik's writing samples are mostly academic and formal, but his personality leaks through in small ways.

His humour is:
- **Dry and observational** — noticing something odd or unexpected and naming it without overreacting
- **Found in facts** — "Platypuses glow under UV light" delivered completely straight, but that IS the joke
- **Rhetorical self-awareness** — "Is it perfection? No." He asks questions he already knows the answer to, then answers them before you can
- **Earned, not performed** — he never announces the joke; the joke is in the precision of the sentence

---

## The key move: subverting the expected

From his Crucible essay:
> "He chose his words carefully here, knowing he had stripped away his reputation — though for a better cause."

That "though for a better cause" is doing quiet comic work. He sets up a terrible thing (destroyed reputation) then undercuts it with calm logic. No winking, no LOL — just the contrast itself.

**In bakery posts this becomes:**
> "Custom eggless cake. Takes 3–5 days. Worth more than one."
> "Yes, there are no eggs. Yes, it still tastes like that."
> "Ordered Monday. Eaten Saturday. Zero leftovers."

The humour is in what's NOT said. The understatement.

---

## What makes him smile (from the samples)

- Unexpected facts stated flatly (platypuses on knuckles, glowing under UV)
- The gap between what people expect and what's actually true
- Precision applied to something seemingly simple ("He says it twice. It's not accidental.")
- Finding the right word when a weaker one would have done
- Things that are more complicated than they look

**In bakery posts:**
- "Eggless doesn't mean less. It means different — and we've spent years getting that difference right."
- "It's a cake. It also happens to have no eggs, no compromise, and a 3-day freshness shelf life."

---

## What his humour is NOT

- ❌ Puns (he's too precise for puns)
- ❌ Self-deprecating ("we're just a small bakery trying our best!")
- ❌ Forced relatability ("we know how stressful birthday planning is!")
- ❌ Pop culture references
- ❌ Exclamation marks as a substitute for wit
- ❌ Sarcasm at anyone's expense
- ❌ Memes or trend-chasing
- ❌ Over-explaining the joke

---

## Rule of thumb

**If you need to signal that something is funny, it isn't.** His humour is invisible until you read it twice. Write the sentence straight. If the contrast, the precision, or the understatement does the work — you've done it right.

**One dry line per post, maximum.** Not every post needs humour. When in doubt, leave it out and let the directness do the work.

---

## Examples of on-brand dry lines for bakery GBP posts

- "No eggs were harmed. No flavour was sacrificed."
- "We've made over 200 custom cakes in Marsden Park. At some point, we stopped being surprised by the orders. At some point."
- "Ordered for a gathering of 8. The cake fed 8. The leftovers fed nobody, because there weren't any."
- "It's called eggless. It doesn't taste like it."
- "Custom cakes available with 3–5 days notice. Miracles available with 7."
