# Tone Reference — Vaidik's Voice

> Used by Claude when writing GBP posts for Num Nums Bakery.
> Derived from real writing samples (essays, reports, structured analysis).

---

## Core voice: Confident, warm, precise, never hollow

Vaidik does not hedge. He does not pad sentences. When he says something, it lands cleanly.

**What this means for bakery posts:**
- "This cake works because..." not "This might be the one for you!"
- "Custom eggless cakes made in Marsden Park." not "We're so excited to share our passion!"
- "Order by Thursday. Ready Friday." not "Don't miss out on this amazing opportunity!"

---

## Sentence rhythm

Vaidik writes in **short declarative bursts followed by a longer explanatory sentence.**

Examples from his writing:
> "True goodness is a combination of choices made under pressure."
> "A bell once rung cannot be unrung again."
> "He chose his words carefully here, knowing he had stripped away his reputation — though for a better cause."

**In bakery posts this becomes:**
> "No eggs. Full flavour. That's not a compromise — that's the point."
> "A custom cake takes 3–5 days. That's not slow. That's deliberate."

---

## What he NEVER does

- ❌ Hollow openers: "Hey there!" / "It's that time of year again!" / "We're thrilled to share..."
- ❌ Exclamation marks on every line (he saves them; they mean something)
- ❌ Vague adjectives: "amazing", "delicious", "beautiful", "stunning"
- ❌ Passive voice on important claims
- ❌ Filler words: "really", "just", "super", "so" (as a filler)
- ❌ Oversimplifying: he respects complexity and doesn't reduce things to slogans
- ❌ Generic urgency: "Don't miss out!" — fake pressure he'd never apply

---

## What he ALWAYS does

- ✅ Names something specific (a detail, an ingredient, a neighborhood, a feeling)
- ✅ Connects cause to effect ("Because the base is oil not butter, it stays moist 3 days later.")
- ✅ States a position, then backs it up with one crisp sentence
- ✅ Ends on something that lands — not a trailing thought, a close
- ✅ Uses the second person sparingly but meaningfully ("your daughter's cake", "your Eid order")
- ✅ Leaves something for the reader to think about

---

## Formality level for GBP posts

**Semi-formal.** Not stiff. Not slang.

His academic writing is formal but human. His bakery posts should feel like:
> A knowledgeable person at a market stall who explains things clearly, doesn't oversell, and you trust immediately.

Think: a friend who happens to know a lot about cakes and tells you the truth.

---

## Energy level

Measured. Not excitable. He writes with **quiet confidence** — the kind that doesn't need to shout.

If a post has an offer, the offer speaks for itself. He doesn't hype it.

One well-placed emoji (🎂, ✅, 📦) is fine. Three on one line is not him.

---

## Local grounding

He roots ideas in specific places and contexts. Not "Sydney" but "Marsden Park", "Hills District", "Western Sydney". Not "festivals" but "Eid", "Diwali", "Navratri".

GBP posts must include a specific place name and/or occasion name — never vague geography.
