# Vocabulary Reference — Vaidik's Word Choices

> Used by Claude when writing GBP posts for Num Nums Bakery.
> Derived from real writing samples across academic essays, structured reports, and analysis.

---

## Words and phrases he uses naturally

### Precision markers
- "deliberately" / "deliberate" — he names intent, doesn't imply it
- "precisely" / "with precision"
- "specifically"
- "directly" — "this directly affects..."
- "consistently"
- "fundamentally"
- "genuinely" — signals sincerity, used sparingly so it lands

### Cause-and-effect connectors
- "which is why..." — his favourite connective
- "this is what X looks like when done right"
- "this demonstrates..."
- "this reveals..."
- "this ensures..."
- "this aligns with..."
- "one [thing] leads to another"

### Contrast and complexity markers
- "however" — used at start of paragraph to introduce counterargument
- "not X — Y" / "not just X; this is Y"
- "whilst" (Australian formal usage, not "while")
- "even though"
- "at what cost"
- "under pressure"

### Strong verbs (he uses these; never the weak versions)
| ✅ He uses | ❌ Not these |
|---|---|
| "destroys" | "damages" / "hurts a bit" |
| "strips away" | "removes" / "loses" |
| "compels" | "makes" / "gets" / "forces" |
| "addresses" | "deals with" / "handles" |
| "reveals" | "shows" / "tells us" |
| "constructs" | "creates" / "puts together" |
| "earns" | "gets" / "deserves" |
| "sacrifices" | "gives up" |

### Quantity and specificity
He always uses **exact numbers** — never vague quantities:
- "48 hours minimum" not "a couple of days"
- "15 flavour varieties" not "many flavours"
- "6-inch serves 6–8 people" not "great for small gatherings"
- "4.6 stars" not "highly rated"
- "since 2019" not "for years"

### Structural phrases for GBP posts
- "Order by [day]. Ready [day]." — clean instruction
- "No [X]. No [Y]." — his version of listing what's absent
- "That's not [negative framing]. That's [reframe]."
- "This is the [noun] most [people] don't [verb]."
- "[Specific detail] — not [vague alternative]."

## Words he NEVER uses

### Hollow adjectives (banned)
- ❌ "amazing" / "wonderful" / "fantastic" / "awesome" / "incredible"
- ❌ "delicious" (too vague — he'd say "moist", "layered", "rich with ganache")
- ❌ "yummy" / "scrumptious" / "divine"
- ❌ "stunning" / "gorgeous" / "beautiful" (for describing cakes)
- ❌ "perfect" — unless used sarcastically or in a very specific context

### Filler words (banned)
- ❌ "really" as an intensifier
- ❌ "just" as a minimiser ("just order", "just call us")
- ❌ "super" ("super fresh", "super moist")
- ❌ "very" — he'd use a stronger word instead

### Marketing clichés (banned)
- ❌ "We're passionate about..."
- ❌ "Made with love" (used on the website, but he wouldn't write it in a GBP post)
- ❌ "journey" (as in "our baking journey")
- ❌ "excited to share" / "thrilled to announce"
- ❌ "your special day" (too generic)
- ❌ "don't miss out"
- ❌ "treat yourself" / "treat your loved ones"
- ❌ "the perfect gift"
- ❌ "order yours today!" (with exclamation)

## Bakery-specific vocabulary (Num Nums context)

### Terms he'd use
- "eggless" — always lowercase in body text, never caps-locked in mid-sentence
- "custom cake" not "personalised cake"
- "order" not "purchase" or "buy" (though "buy" is fine in offers)
- "sponge" not "cake layers" or "batter"
- "buttercream" / "fondant" / "ganache" — uses technical terms correctly
- "fresh to order" not "freshly baked" (implies it's not pre-made)
- "pickup" not "collection"
- "48-hour notice" not "advance notice required"
- "flavour" not "flavor" (Australian spelling)
- "moist" — the specific texture claim he'd make over "soft" or "fluffy"

### Occasion and community vocabulary
He names celebrations specifically, never generically:
- ✅ "Eid", "Diwali", "Navratri", "Janmashtami", "Raksha Bandhan", "Ganesh Chaturthi"
- ✅ "Jain-friendly", "vegetarian", "halal-friendly"
- ✅ "Harris Park", "Marsden Park", "Riverstone", "Hills District", "Western Sydney", "Parramatta"
- ❌ "upcoming festivities" / "your special celebration" / "cultural event"

### Price and size language
- "Starts at $39.99 for a 6-inch" — always gives the anchor
- "Serves 6–8" — always gives a people count alongside size
- "Photo cake add-on: $19.99" — cost as a specific fact, never "small additional fee"

## The sentence pattern he defaults to

**Claim → specific evidence → implication**

> "The bakery opened in Harris Park for a reason. One of Sydney's largest South Asian communities lives there. That's not coincidence — that's intent."

> "48 hours minimum. That's what fresh baking actually takes."

> "No eggs. Not in the sponge, not in the buttercream, not in any of the 15 flavours. That's the point."

---

## Register check before publishing any post

Ask: does this sentence contain any of the banned words or patterns?
If yes — rewrite. One strong specific word beats three vague positive ones every time.
