Eggless Cakes in Northwest Sydney: Serving Schofields, The Ponds, Box Hill & More

Northwest Sydney is the fastest-growing residential corridor in New South Wales. Over the past decade, a belt of new suburbs stretching from Schofields and The Ponds through Marsden Park, Box Hill, and Tallawong has transformed from paddocks and semi-rural estates into one of Sydney's most populated and culturally diverse areas. Hundreds of thousands of people now call this corridor home.

And yet one thing has been conspicuously absent: a dedicated, 100% eggless cake bakery serving the whole area. Until now.

Num Num's Bakery at Riverstone Shopping Centre is the north-west's anchor eggless cake shop — positioned at the geographic centre of the growth corridor, within 8–22 minutes of every major suburb. The Northwest Sydney Eggless Cake Problem

Let's be direct about what's missing in northwest Sydney: there is no other dedicated eggless cake bakery in the entire corridor. If you live in Schofields, The Ponds, Box Hill, Tallawong, or Marsden Park and you want a properly eggless custom cake — not a "we can try to substitute" cake, not a generic supermarket product labelled "suitable for vegetarians" — your options are extremely limited without travelling to Parramatta or Harris Park.

That gap matters for a large and growing section of the north-western population. An estimated 30–40% of residents in this corridor come from South Asian, Sri Lankan, South-East Asian, or Middle Eastern backgrounds where eggs are excluded from the diet for religious, cultural, or personal reasons. Hindu vegetarians, Jain households, and families observing specific dietary practices all face the same problem at every birthday party, naming ceremony, Diwali gathering, and celebration: where do you get a cake that everyone at the table can eat?

Num Num's Bakery at Riverstone was built specifically to answer that question. And because it sits at the northern end of Richmond Road — accessible from virtually every suburb in the corridor — it functions as the region's shared eggless bakery, even if residents of each suburb think of it as "their local shop."

Why Eggless Matters in the Northwest Growth Corridor

The northwest corridor is not just growing in population — it is growing in diversity. New housing estates attract buyers from across Sydney's western and south-western suburbs, where South Asian communities have been established for decades. Many of these buyers are raising their first families, hosting their first community celebrations, and establishing the cultural rhythms of their new neighbourhoods.

For families where eggs are excluded — whether for Hindu vegetarian reasons, Jain dietary codes, Islamic halal concerns around egg sourcing, or personal preference — the question of where to find a reliable, high-quality eggless cake is not occasional. It comes up at every birthday, every baby shower, every festival. A cake that requires no explanation, no label-checking, and no second-guessing is genuinely valuable in a community context.

At Num Num's Bakery, there is no second-guessing. Every product is 100% eggless — not a modified version of an egg-containing recipe, but a recipe developed from the ground up to produce the same moist, flavoursome result without eggs. The difference in everyday use is meaningful: you order a cake, your guests eat the cake. No exclusions, no alternatives needed.
