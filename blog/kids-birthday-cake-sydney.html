Why Does an Eggless Cake Make Sense for Kids' Parties?

Egg is one of the most common childhood food allergies, affecting roughly 9% of Australian infants (Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia). At a party of fifteen children, the odds that at least one avoids eggs are genuinely high — and unlike adults, kids can't always advocate for themselves at the table. An eggless cake removes that risk before it becomes a problem.

The wider picture backs it up: in 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence found 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — now live with allergic disease, double the 2007 figure (NACE, Costly Reactions report, August 2025). For parents, an eggless cake is the simplest way to make sure the birthday photo includes every child with a slice in front of them — not a couple watching from the side.

What Are the Best Cake Flavours for a Kids' Birthday?

From our internal order data, kids' birthday cakes skew hard towards Chocolate, Cookies & Cream, and the bright fruit flavours — Strawberry, Mango, and Pineapple. Children tend to want familiar and fun over sophisticated, so the showstopper adult flavours like Tiramisu rarely feature. The trick is picking something the birthday child loves that the other kids will happily eat too.

A pattern we see often: the safest crowd-pleaser for a mixed group of kids is Chocolate or Vanilla, but the birthday child usually wants the cake to match a theme — pink for a unicorn party, blue for superheroes. Our tip is to choose a kid-friendly flavour first, then let the design carry the theme. That way the cake looks like the party and still tastes like something every child will finish.

What Size Birthday Cake Do You Need for a Kids' Party?

From our experience, kids' parties are where sizing trips people up most — children eat smaller slices, but there are usually more mouths than expected once siblings and parents are counted. As a rule, size for the total headcount, not just the kids, and round up so there's enough for seconds and a slice to send home.

  • 6-inch round — 8–10 portions, a small party or a cake just for the family
  • 8-inch round — 15–20 portions, the classic kids'-party size
  • 10-inch round — 30–40 portions, a big class or extended-family party
  • Two-tier — 40-plus, for a milestone like a first or tenth birthday

If you tell us how many children and adults are coming when you message, we'll suggest the right size. The full sizing guide also lives on the Order Online page, and you can browse finished cakes on the Our Cakes page for a sense of scale.

Cake or Cupcakes — What's Better for a Kids' Party?

From our order data, it's roughly an even split, and the right choice depends on how the party runs. A single birthday cake is the centrepiece — it's what the candles go on, what gets sung over, and what makes the photo. Cupcakes win on practicality: no cutting, no plates, easy for little hands, and simple to hand out so every child gets exactly one. Many parents order both — a small feature cake for the moment, cupcakes for the crowd.

For a party at a venue or park where serving needs to be quick, cupcakes are hard to beat. For an at-home celebration where there's time to cut and plate, a cake gives you more impact for the photos. Are Kids' Birthday Cakes Safe for Allergies at a Party?

Here's the honest answer parents deserve. Every Num Num's cake is made in a 100% egg-free kitchen, which removes the egg cross-contamination pathway — a real benefit, since egg is a priority allergen that must be declared on Australian food labels (Food Standards Australia New Zealand). For an egg allergy or an egg-free diet, an eggless cake is a genuine solution.

But eggless does not mean allergen-free in every sense. Our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter), may involve nuts, and standard cakes contain wheat. So an eggless cake is not automatically safe for a child with a dairy, nut, or gluten allergy. If any guest has an allergy beyond eggs, tell us when you order and we'll give you a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour.

For a severe or anaphylactic allergy, please state your child's needs clearly when ordering and follow your own medical and ASCIA-aligned advice. We're glad to talk through ingredients so you can make the safest decision for your party — we just won't promise medical-grade safety we can't guarantee.
