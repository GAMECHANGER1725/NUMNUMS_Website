What Makes a Great Celebration Cake?

A great celebration cake balances three things: it tastes excellent, it looks like the occasion, and it suits everyone at the table. The first two are craft; the third is the one people forget until a guest quietly puts their plate down. A cake that's eggless from the first ingredient solves that problem before it starts — nobody has to ask what's inside.

The taste comes from treating eggless baking as a discipline, not a substitution. We rebuild each recipe so the crumb is soft and moist without eggs, then finish with fresh cream, fondant or buttercream as the design needs. The result reads as a premium cake, full stop — which is the only standard worth setting for a once-a-year moment.

In 2025, 30% of Australians — 8.2 million people — lived with allergic disease, double the 19.6% recorded in 2007 (NACE, August 2025). For a Sydney celebration drawn from many backgrounds, a 100% eggless cake is the one centrepiece that includes the whole guest list.

Why Are Num Num's Celebration Cakes 100% Eggless?

Because eggless is the most inclusive way to make a celebration cake, and inclusion is the whole point of a shared cake. Egg allergy affects roughly 9% of Australian infants (Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia), and millions of adults avoid eggs on cultural or religious grounds. One eggless cake quietly covers them all.

Crucially, our entire kitchen is egg-free — no eggs in any product, prep area or equipment. According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), eggs are a priority allergen, and cross-contamination from a mixed kitchen is a genuine risk. A dedicated eggless bakery removes that risk by design — which matters most at a big event where you can't vet every guest's needs in advance.

There's a subtle quality payoff too. Because a dedicated eggless recipe is engineered around a stable crumb, our cakes hold up well through the long middle of an event — the speeches, the photos, the slow march to cake-cutting — where an egg sponge can dry once it's been sitting out and cut. For a celebration that runs for hours, that staying power is part of why the cake still tastes fresh at the end.

Are Num Num's Celebration Cakes Suitable for Every Dietary Need?

Egg-free covers a wide range of the dietary needs at a Sydney celebration. Because eggs are one of Australia's priority allergens under FSANZ labelling rules, an entirely egg-free kitchen removes the cross-contamination risk that comes with a mixed bakery.

Our egg-free kitchen suits:

  • Egg-allergic guests — no eggs in any product, prep area or equipment on-site.
  • Hindu vegetarian and Jain households — both traditions commonly exclude eggs.
  • Many Sikh families — some Sikh dietary practices exclude eggs.
  • Halal-observant households — our egg-free kitchen sidesteps cross-contamination concerns.
  • Vegan note: our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter), so they're egg-free but not vegan — see vegan vs eggless cakes.
