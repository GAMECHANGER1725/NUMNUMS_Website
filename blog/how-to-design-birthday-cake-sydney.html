How to Design a Birthday Cake in Sydney: A Complete Guide

By Tarun Patel 27 June 2026 12 min read Custom Cakes · Birthday

Designing a birthday cake in Sydney should be one of the most enjoyable parts of planning a celebration — a chance to translate someone's personality, favourite flavours, and the theme of the occasion into something edible, beautiful, and genuinely memorable. In practice, though, most Sydney families find the process more overwhelming than exciting. How big should the cake be? Which flavour will everyone actually eat? Should you go with fondant or buttercream? How do you brief a bakery so you get exactly what you imagined? And if half your guest list avoids eggs for religious, cultural, or allergy reasons, how do you find a bakery where the entire menu is already safe for everyone?

This guide walks through every decision in the birthday cake design process — from choosing the right size for your party to selecting flavours, planning decorations, and understanding what to include in your order brief. At Num Num's Bakery, we take custom birthday cake orders across Sydney from our two locations in Harris Park and Riverstone, and every cake on our menu — across all 15 flavours and 6 sizes — is 100% eggless as standard. Whether you're planning an intimate family dinner or a 60-person milestone celebration, this guide will help you arrive at the perfect cake with confidence.

How Do You Choose the Right Cake Size?

The single most common mistake people make when ordering a birthday cake in Sydney is getting the size wrong — and it almost always goes in the direction of too small rather than too large. People consistently underestimate how many servings a cake needs to comfortably cover a birthday party when guests are also eating other food, desserts, or when children are present and want a meaningful slice rather than a sliver.

At Num Num's Bakery, we offer 6 standard round cake sizes. Here is a practical serving guide that reflects real-world party conditions rather than theoretical maximums:

6-inch — 8 to 10 serves
Ideal for intimate birthday dinners, family of 6–8, or as a personal celebration cake. Perfect when you want a beautiful presentation without large quantities. Great for 1st birthdays where only 1–2 adults are eating the actual cake.
8-inch — 12 to 16 serves
The most popular size for birthday parties in Sydney. Works well for groups of 12–16 adults, or a children's party where 18–20 kids will be sharing with parents. The sweet spot between presence and practicality.
10-inch — 20 to 25 serves
A genuinely impressive tier for parties of 20–28 people. If you're hosting a combined birthday and get-together with family and friends, a 10-inch is typically the right call without needing to go tiered.
12-inch — 30 to 35 serves
For milestone birthdays — 30th, 40th, 50th — with a guest list of 30–40 people. A 12-inch cake has real visual weight and presence when it comes to the table, without needing a multi-tier arrangement.
14-inch — 40 to 50 serves
Suitable for larger functions of 40–55 guests. This size works well for hall events, combined family celebrations, and corporate birthday functions where you need plenty of generous slices.
16-inch — 55 to 65 serves
Our largest single-tier option. For parties of 55–70 guests, or when you want maximum impact for a large milestone birthday. For parties beyond 70 guests, a two-tier combination is the recommended approach.

A general rule: if your party has mixed ages (adults and children), count everyone as a full serve. Children eat as much cake as adults at birthday parties — often more. If you are serving other desserts alongside the cake, you may be able to go one size down, but when in doubt, go larger. Leftover birthday cake is never a problem; too little is.

Which Flavour Should You Choose for a Birthday Cake?

Choosing a birthday cake flavour is both easier and harder than most people expect. Easier, because there is usually one clear front-runner if you think about who you are celebrating and what they genuinely love. Harder, because birthday cakes are almost always eaten by a group, and a flavour that one person loves intensely — like a very strong coffee tiramisu — might not land with every guest at the table.

At Num Num's Bakery, we offer 15 eggless flavours, and our recommendation is to prioritise the birthday person's preference first and then consider the guest list as a secondary filter. Here's a practical overview of our full flavour range with honest guidance on who each tends to suit:

  • Chocolate — our most universally loved flavour. Rich, deep, and crowd-pleasing across every age group and cultural background. If you're unsure, Chocolate is the safe and genuinely delicious choice.
  • Vanilla — deceptively simple but technically demanding to do well. Ours is fragrant and not overly sweet. Suits children's parties and guests who prefer a lighter finish.
  • Red Velvet — dramatic deep red sponge with a cream cheese-adjacent frosting. Visually striking for photos and milestone birthdays. Widely popular with teens and young adults.
  • Butterscotch — warm, caramel-forward, and nostalgic. A standout choice for South Asian celebrations where this flavour carries strong sentimental associations.
  • Black Forest — cherry, chocolate, and cream in classic proportions. Suits adults who appreciate a less sweet, more sophisticated profile.
  • White Forest — a lighter, creamier variation on the Black Forest theme. A good choice for summer birthday parties and guests who find dark chocolate too intense.
  • Strawberry — fresh and fruity, ideal for children's parties and spring or summer celebrations. Pairs beautifully with pink decoration themes.
  • Blueberry — a more subtle berry flavour with a moist crumb. Popular for milestone birthdays where the guest of honour wants something a little different from the standard options.
  • Mango — tropical, bright, and genuinely popular across South Asian and East Asian communities. A standout choice for summer birthdays.
  • Lychee — delicate, floral, and elegant. Consistently popular with guests who prefer lighter flavours, particularly within Chinese and Korean communities.
  • Pineapple — tangy and refreshing. A surprising crowd-pleaser for parties where you want something a little unexpected.
  • Ferrero Rocher — our most indulgent flavour. Hazelnut, chocolate, and luxury. A consistent top choice for milestone adult birthdays.
  • Tiramisu — coffee-forward and sophisticated. For adults who don't want a "sweet" cake but want something complex and memorable.
  • Cookies & Cream — playful and reliably loved by children, teenagers, and young adults. A birthday party staple.
  • Rasmalai — South Asian-inspired, fragrant with cardamom, saffron, and rose. Deeply meaningful for cultural milestones and Diwali celebrations.

How Do Fillings and Tiers Change the Cake Design?

Once you've chosen your flavour, the next structural decision is whether you want a single-tier cake or a multi-tier arrangement, and what the internal filling layers should look like. These two decisions have more impact on the final eating experience than almost any other factor — and they are often underweighted in the ordering conversation.

Single-tier vs multi-tier

A single-tier birthday cake is the right choice for parties of up to 35–40 guests when using our larger sizes (12"–14"). It is simpler to transport, easier to cut at the table, and usually more cost-effective. A multi-tier cake — most commonly a two-tier arrangement combining a 10" base with a 6" or 8" top — adds visual drama and enables you to offer two different flavours, which is increasingly popular for birthday parties where the guest list has mixed preferences. If you want Chocolate on the lower tier and Mango on the upper, or Ferrero Rocher base with Vanilla top, a two-tier arrangement delivers that without requiring two separate cakes.

Filling and layering

The internal filling of a Num Num's Bakery cake is composed of alternating sponge layers and cream filling, with the flavour infused throughout both elements. For most flavours, the sponge is flavoured and the cream filling carries complementary elements — fruit compote for fruit flavours, chocolate ganache for Chocolate and Ferrero Rocher, coffee soak for Tiramisu. When briefing your order, you can specify any preference for lighter or richer cream layers, or ask for fresh fruit additions where the flavour supports it.

For milestone birthdays — 18th, 21st, 30th, 50th — we recommend considering a richer filling combination. A Ferrero Rocher cake with a hazelnut cream and crushed praline layer, for example, has a genuinely luxurious eating experience that matches the occasion. What Decoration Options Are Available for Birthday Cakes?

Decoration is where a birthday cake becomes unmistakably personal. The same Chocolate cake in an 8" round can look like a simple family dinner dessert or a professionally styled centrepiece depending on what happens on the outside. At Num Num's Bakery, we offer four main decoration approaches, and each suits different birthday party styles.

Buttercream finishes

Buttercream is our most popular finish for birthday cakes in Sydney, and for good reason — it is versatile, edible, and visually warm in a way that fondant can struggle to match. We offer smooth buttercream, textured palette-knife finishes, rosette piping, and watercolour effects using natural food colouring. Buttercream works across all 15 flavours and creates a soft, approachable look ideal for children's parties, family celebrations, and any event where the aesthetic is warm rather than formal. For a deeper comparison of the two finishes, see our post on eggless cake buttercream vs fondant.

Fondant finishes

Fondant creates a smooth, polished surface that allows for precise printed details, sharp edges, and three-dimensional sculpted elements. It is the right choice when the theme demands structural decorations — a character cake for a child's birthday, a tiered formal cake for a 21st or 30th, or any design that involves complex figurines or architectural elements on top of the cake. Fondant travels better than buttercream in warm weather and is the preferred choice when the cake needs to sit out at room temperature for extended periods during a celebration.

Photo cakes (edible printing)

Edible photo printing places a high-resolution image directly onto the surface of the cake using food-safe ink on an edible rice paper base. This technique works across all our flavours and all our sizes from 8" and above. It is particularly popular for milestone birthdays — a 50th with a collage of family photos, a 21st with a custom graphic, a child's birthday with their favourite cartoon character printed in vivid detail. To order a photo cake, WhatsApp your image to +61 425 697 725 in the highest resolution available (at least 1200 × 1200 pixels for best results).

Theme cakes and custom decorations

Theme cakes bring an entire visual world onto the birthday table — sports jerseys, floral gardens, galaxy prints, characters from children's shows, travel themes, cultural motifs. Our team works from a detailed brief you provide via WhatsApp: a description of the theme, reference images if you have them, the colour palette, and any text you want on the cake. The more specific your brief, the closer the result will be to your vision. Theme cakes require a minimum of 4–5 days notice rather than the standard 48 hours, so plan accordingly. If your child wants a princess castle or a dinosaur volcano, message us early to ensure we have the time to do it justice.

Why Does Eggless Matter for Birthday Parties in Sydney?

Sydney is one of the most culturally diverse cities in the world, and birthday parties in Sydney regularly bring together guests from multiple cultural and religious backgrounds at the same table. In many households — Hindu, Jain, Buddhist, and others — eggs are excluded from the diet as a matter of religious practice, not preference. In others, a guest may have an egg allergy. In others still, the person being celebrated is vegetarian and would find it strange to cut into a cake made with eggs when their own diet excludes them.

The problem with conventional birthday cakes from most Sydney bakeries is that they require you to ask the question. "Does this cake have eggs?" creates a moment of awkwardness — and for egg-allergic children, it creates a genuine safety concern. At Num Num's Bakery, the answer is never in doubt: every single product on the menu, every flavour, every size, every decoration finish, is made without eggs. The kitchen contains no eggs at any point in the production process. This is not a "we can do a special eggless version" situation — it is the entire bakery.

For a comprehensive exploration of why eggless birthday cakes matter for inclusive celebrations, see our full post on eggless birthday cakes in Sydney. The data on egg allergy prevalence in Australia is striking: according to Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia, egg allergy affects approximately 9% of Australian infants — making it the most common food allergy in children under five. And beyond allergy, the egg-avoidance community in Sydney's western suburbs is substantial: the 2021 ABS Census shows that Harris Park, Parramatta, and the surrounding Western Sydney suburbs have among the highest proportions of residents of Indian origin in Australia, a community where egg avoidance for religious reasons is mainstream rather than exceptional.

Planning an eggless birthday party is also about the feeling of inclusion. When every guest can eat the same birthday cake without exception, without checking, without asking, without worrying — that is when a celebration genuinely brings people together. That's the experience Num Num's Bakery is built to deliver for every birthday in Sydney we're part of.

What Is the Planning Timeline for a Birthday Cake in Sydney?

The most consistent feedback we receive from first-time customers is: "I wish I'd ordered earlier." Sydney's custom cake market moves quickly, especially around school holidays, long weekends, and major cultural celebrations. Here is a realistic planning timeline based on our experience taking thousands of birthday cake orders across Sydney:

  • 48 hours minimum: The absolute minimum notice for a standard birthday cake with a straightforward decoration brief — a smooth buttercream finish with a personalised message and basic colour scheme. This is our hard minimum across both locations.
  • 4–5 days: Recommended for theme cakes, photo cakes, multi-tier arrangements, or any design that involves sculpted fondant decorations or complex visual elements. This gives our team time to source any required materials and ensure the execution is right.
  • 1 week or more: Recommended during July school holidays, October school holidays, December (Christmas and end-of-year parties), and around major cultural celebrations including Diwali, Eid, and Easter. Demand during these periods is extremely high.
  • 2+ weeks: If you want a very specific, elaborate design — a sculpted cake, a fully custom tiered design, or a very large order for 50+ guests — giving us 2 weeks or more allows us to plan and deliver at the highest quality level.

The key thing to remember is that our cakes are baked fresh to order. We do not maintain a stock of pre-made cakes waiting for collection. This is a deliberate choice that ensures freshness and quality, but it means planning ahead is essential. A birthday on a Saturday is best ordered by Wednesday at the latest; a milestone birthday in the school holidays should be ordered at least a week in advance.

According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), food businesses have specific obligations around allergen labelling and declaration — which is another reason we take written confirmation of every order so seriously. Every Num Num's Bakery birthday cake is accompanied by written confirmation of its ingredients so you have full clarity on allergen status for your guests.

How Do You Write the Perfect Cake Brief?

A good cake brief makes the difference between a cake that matches your vision and one that approximates it. After thousands of birthday cake orders, here are the seven elements that make a brief work:

  1. Size: State the size in inches (6", 8", 10", 12", 14", or 16") or the approximate number of guests if you'd like us to recommend.
  2. Flavour: Your chosen flavour from our 15-option menu. If you want two flavours (for a two-tier cake), specify which goes on which tier.
  3. Base decoration style: Smooth buttercream, textured buttercream, fondant, or edible photo print.
  4. Colour palette: List your colours specifically — "dusty rose and gold" is more useful than "pink." Include a reference image if possible.
  5. Theme or design elements: Describe any specific decorations, characters, flowers, geometric patterns, or other visual elements you want included.
  6. Text on the cake: The exact wording — name, age, message — spelled correctly. We transcribe exactly what you send.
  7. Pick-up date, time, and location: Harris Park or Riverstone, with your preferred collection window. We'll confirm the exact time.

The more complete your brief, the less back-and-forth is needed and the more confident we can be that the finished cake matches your expectations. Reference images are extremely helpful for any non-standard design. You can send multiple images to convey different elements — "I want the flowers from this image but the colour palette from this other one" is a perfectly valid brief.
