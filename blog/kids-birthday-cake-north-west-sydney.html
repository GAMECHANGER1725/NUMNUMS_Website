What Do North-West Sydney Parents Look for in a Kids' Birthday Cake?

Are Num Num's Kids' Birthday Cakes Safe for School and Daycare Parties?

Every kids' birthday cake we bake comes from a 100% egg-free kitchen, so egg allergy is never a cross-contamination risk. That's a genuine relief for school and daycare parties, where a class list often includes at least one child with an egg allergy. That said, honesty matters more here than anywhere else on the menu. Our cakes do contain dairy, and depending on the design may involve nuts or wheat. Egg-free is not the same as allergen-free.

Under Australia's Food Standards Code, the ten priority food allergens must be clearly declared on food labels. That list includes egg, milk and tree nuts, according to Food Standards Australia New Zealand. We follow the same standard when we talk you through ingredients before a school party order. We'd rather ask twice about allergies than guess once. For children with a severe or multiple allergy, always check current guidance from ASCIA and confirm with your child's school or daycare before the cake is served.

A pattern worth knowing if you're ordering for a classroom: schools and daycares in the north-west increasingly ask for a full ingredient list. A verbal assurance is no longer enough. We keep this on file for every kids' cake order. It saves a scramble the morning of the party.

What Size Kids' Birthday Cake Do You Need by Age and Guest Count?

Size depends far more on guest count than on the birthday child's age. It's the question we field most from North-West Sydney parents booking a party. A 10-inch or two-tier cake covers 30-40 guests for a bigger school-age celebration.
