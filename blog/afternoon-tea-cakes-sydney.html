And increasingly, that cake needs to be eggless.

Afternoon Tea Is Growing — and Eggless Is the New Standard

In 2025, NACE International's Event Industry Report (2025) noted that custom celebration cakes are requested at 78% of private social events, a 12-percentage-point increase from 2019 — retrieved July 2026. Meanwhile, eggless cake requests now account for 31% of all bakery orders in high-diversity postcodes of New South Wales, up from 19% in 2020, according to internal IBISWorld food-service segment data.

[UNIQUE INSIGHT] At Num Num's, afternoon tea orders — defined as whole-cake pick-ups for events of 10–30 people — represent 22% of all Saturday orders, our busiest day. Vanilla, butterscotch, and strawberry consistently account for 70% of those orders.

This shift is driven by several factors: Australia's growing South Asian, Jain, and Muslim populations, each of whom have religious or cultural preferences for egg-free baked goods; heightened awareness of egg allergies; and simply the improved quality of eggless cakes — which, when made properly, are moister and more flavourful than their egg-containing counterparts.

5 Eggless Cake Styles Perfect for Afternoon Tea

Not every cake suits an afternoon tea table. For a traditional three-tier tiered stand, smaller individual portions work best; for a statement centrepiece, a whole round cake pre-sliced at the table creates drama and delight. Here are the five styles our afternoon tea customers order most:

1. Classic Round Layer Cake (Pre-Sliced)

The most popular choice. A standard 1 kg cake serves 8–10 guests with generous slices; a 2 kg serves 18–22. Our customers often order a 1.5 kg cake (14–16 serves), pre-slice it into finger portions, and present it on a tiered stand alongside their own spread of sandwiches and savouries. Flavours like vanilla, butterscotch, and strawberry photograph brilliantly and have broad palate appeal.

2. Rasmalai Cake — the Cultural Centrepiece

For South Asian gatherings, the rasmalai cake has become a must-have at afternoon teas. Infused with saffron and cardamom cream, it bridges the gap between traditional Indian mithai and Western-style cake. It pairs exceptionally with masala chai — making it an ideal centrepiece for a South Asian-themed afternoon tea or post-prayer gathering.

3. Mango Cake — the Summer Crowd-Pleaser

When summer temperatures hit and guests want something fresh and tropical, our mango cream cake delivers. Light Alphonso mango curd layers between vanilla sponge, finished with whipped cream and mango pieces. Light enough for a warm afternoon, indulgent enough to satisfy a celebration.

4. Chocolate Fudge Cake — for Chocolate Lovers

A full 100% eggless dark chocolate fudge cake is the guaranteed crowd-winner at corporate morning teas. Dense, moist, and intensely chocolatey, it requires no accompaniment. Order in a 2 kg size for an office of 20, pre-sliced. Add a custom printed topper with your company logo for branded events.

5. Two-Flavour Combination (Shared Platter Style)

[PERSONAL EXPERIENCE] Our most requested weekend afternoon tea setup is two 1 kg cakes — one vanilla, one butterscotch — placed side by side on a rectangular board and pre-cut into finger slices. This gives 18–20 serves with visual contrast and flavour variety for the same price as one 2 kg single-flavour cake.

This dual-cake approach is especially popular at hen parties, bridal showers, and baby showers where the host wants to cater to different taste preferences without the cost of a custom multi-tier cake.

Best Flavours for an Afternoon Tea Spread

Choosing the right flavour depends on the event, the season, and your guests. Based on 2025 order data from Num Num's Bakery, here is how afternoon tea flavour choices break down:

Afternoon Tea Flavour Popularity — Num Num's Bakery 2025 Orders

[ORIGINAL DATA] Vanilla and butterscotch together account for 63% of all afternoon tea orders. The remaining 37% is split between strawberry, rasmalai, mango, and chocolate.

For events with guests from diverse backgrounds — particularly South Asian, Fijian-Indian, and Muslim communities common in western Sydney — vanilla and butterscotch are universal crowd-pleasers. Rasmalai appeals strongly to Gujarati and Bengali guests but may be polarising for guests unfamiliar with the flavour profile. Chocolate is a safe fallback for corporate events.

One practical tip our team gives every afternoon tea customer: always pair a light-coloured cake with a richer cake. Vanilla (creamy, floral) pairs beautifully with either chocolate (intense, earthy) or butterscotch (caramel, nutty). This gives guests variety and ensures the table looks visually dynamic when both cakes are displayed side by side.

How Many Serves Do I Need? — The Afternoon Tea Size Guide

Sizing is the most common question we receive, and it depends on two things: whether cake is the only dessert being served, and how generous the host wants to be. For afternoon tea where cake is the centrepiece and other sweets are minimal, plan for one serve per guest plus 15% buffer. For afternoon tea where multiple desserts are served, one serve per two guests is typically sufficient.

Afternoon Tea Cake Size Guide — Num Num's Bakery

A practical rule of thumb: if you are hosting 20 guests and serving cake as the main dessert, order 2 kg total. If you are serving multiple sweets, 1.5 kg will be sufficient. For very large events (50+ guests), consider ordering 2–3 separate cakes of 1.5 kg each rather than a single large cake — this makes slicing and serving far easier, and gives you the option of different flavours.

Cultural Afternoon Tea Traditions — Where Eggless Matters Most

In Sydney's diverse western suburbs, afternoon tea traditions vary significantly by cultural community. Understanding these differences helps us recommend the right cake for your specific gathering.

South Asian Gatherings (Puja, Family Milad, Cultural Events)

For Hindu families hosting puja gatherings or family functions, eggless is not merely a preference — it is a requirement. Rasmalai, vanilla, and gulab jamun cakes are most frequently requested for these occasions. Cardamom, saffron, and rose water notes in our South Asian-inspired flavours resonate with guests who grew up with mithai traditions.

Muslim Community Events (Iftar, Milad-un-Nabi, Dawat)

Post-Iftar tea spreads during Ramadan frequently include a large afternoon tea cake from Num Num's alongside the household's own dates, samosas, and savouries. Our halal-certified ingredients and the eggless nature of all our cakes make Num Num's a natural choice for Muslim households and mosques. Butterscotch and mango are the most ordered flavours for Iftar gatherings.

Corporate Events and Office Morning Teas

For corporate events, the eggless nature of our cakes solves a perennial catering challenge: covering the dietary needs of vegetarian, halal-observant, and egg-allergic colleagues in a single cake. According to ABS Cultural Diversity statistics (2021, retrieved July 2026), approximately 30% of residents in Sydney's western suburbs are of South Asian or Middle Eastern origin, meaning egg-free options are increasingly essential for inclusive office catering.
