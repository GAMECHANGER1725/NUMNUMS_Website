Why Are So Many Families in This Area Looking for Eggless Kids Cakes?

Marsden Park and Schofields rank among Australia's fastest-growing new suburbs — the Blacktown LGA, which includes both estates, added more than 30,000 residents in the five years to 2021 according to the ABS Regional Population release. That growth has been overwhelmingly young, family-oriented, and multicultural. A large share of new residents come from Indian, Sri Lankan, Bangladeshi, and other South Asian backgrounds where Hindu vegetarianism, Jain dietary practice, or personal preference means eggs don't belong in food. They're not asking for a substitution. They want a bakery that simply doesn't use eggs at all.

That's precisely what Num Num's is. We didn't start as a conventional bakery that added an eggless line. Eggless baking is the only thing we do. Every flavour — Chocolate, Vanilla, Rasmalai, Ferrero Rocher, all 15 of them — has never contained eggs. When a family from Marsden Park orders a Spiderman birthday cake for their six-year-old, every child at the party eats the same cake without any questions asked.

We've noticed something interesting from orders over the past year: parents in the Marsden Park and Schofields catchment tend to order larger cakes than the Sydney average. The estates here have big families and big gatherings. An 8-inch single tier that serves 15–20 people is our most common size across Sydney generally, but from this area we regularly make 10-inch and 12-inch cakes, and two-tier setups for kids' parties that double as community events. The culture here is generous — and we love it.

What Kids Cake Designs Can You Actually Order?

The most common question we get from parents is: "Can you do [character]?" The honest answer is almost always yes — as long as you give us a reference image and enough lead time. Here's what we make most often for kids in this area, and what's involved in each.

Character Cakes (Spiderman, Elsa, Bluey, Paw Patrol)

Character cakes are our most requested kids design by a wide margin. Spiderman is consistently the most popular for boys — followed closely by Bluey, which crosses gender lines completely. For girls, Elsa and Frozen themes dominate, with Barbie gaining ground rapidly through 2025 and into 2026. Paw Patrol stays strong for the 3–5 age group regardless of season.

These designs typically use a combination of fondant work, edible image printing, or hand-piped buttercream to bring the character to life. Send us a reference photo of the design you have in mind — something you've seen on Instagram or Pinterest — and we'll tell you exactly what's achievable within your timeline and budget.

Dinosaur Cakes

Dinosaur themes have seen a real surge over the past two years, particularly for boys aged 4–8. Our dinosaur cakes typically feature textured buttercream in earthy greens and browns, with fondant dinosaur toppers or moulded figures. Some parents go all in with a erupting volcano cake — we can do that too, though it requires at least 5 days notice.

Rainbow and Unicorn Cakes

Rainbow cakes come in two versions: the striking rainbow-layer interior (you cut the cake and reveal layers of colour) or a rainbow-decorated exterior with colourful buttercream swirls. Unicorn cakes combine a spiral horn topper with a palette of pastels — usually lavender, blush, and mint. Both are consistently popular for girls' birthdays in the 5–10 age range.

Smash Cakes for 1st Birthdays

First birthday smash cakes deserve their own mention. A smash cake is a small individual cake — usually a 4-inch or 5-inch single tier — placed in front of the birthday baby for a photo moment before the main cake is cut. We make these in softer flavours (Vanilla, Strawberry, Butterscotch) with gentle frosting the child can grab into without making an absolute disaster. They're eggless, obviously. And they pair beautifully with a matching main cake for the guests.

What Flavour Should You Choose for a Kids Birthday Cake?

Flavour is the question parents agonise over most — and honestly, you can't go wrong. That said, here's what our most experienced opinion looks like after making thousands of kids birthday cakes.

Kids in the 5–10 range almost always vote for Chocolate when asked, but their parents often override with Vanilla because it photographs better against bright frosting. Both are completely valid. What we've found from years of custom orders is that Butterscotch tends to produce the best reactions at the table — it's familiar but not ordinary, warm and caramel-like, and it pairs beautifully with pink or peach buttercream. Strawberry is the third reliable pick, especially for girls' birthday parties.

The Five Best Flavours for Kids Parties

1
Chocolate

The crowd-pleaser. Rich, moist, and pairs with almost any frosting colour. Every child knows they like it — no convincing required.

2
Vanilla

Versatile and photograph-friendly. Works with any themed frosting — pink for Elsa, blue for Bluey, red for Spiderman. A blank canvas that lets the design shine.

3

A warm, caramel-forward flavour that adults love and kids can't quite identify — they just know they want more. One of our best-reviewed cakes for birthday parties.

4

Light and fruity. Especially popular for girls' parties through summer. The pink crumb looks stunning when the cake is sliced and pairs perfectly with cream cheese frosting.

5
Cookies & Cream

A newer addition that's become a genuine favourite for older kids (8–12) and teens. The biscuit pieces in the batter give it a texture that feels different from a standard cake — in a very good way.

How Far in Advance Do You Need to Order?

Standard custom birthday cakes at Num Num's Bakery require a minimum of 48 hours lead time — enough for a single-tier character cake with standard frosting. Elaborate multi-tier designs or orders during NSW school holidays need 4–5 days minimum, and popular school-holiday dates fill 2–3 weeks in advance. Here's how the timeline breaks down so you don't miss out.

48 Hours — Standard Characters and Single Tier

If you're ordering a single-tier cake (6-inch, 8-inch, or 10-inch) with a recognisable character design and a clear reference image, 48 hours is sufficient in most periods. The caveat is school holidays, long weekends, and festive periods — during those windows, the queue fills up quickly and 48 hours may not be enough to get your preferred date.

4–5 Days — Multi-Tier, Fondant Figurines, or Complex Scenes

Two-tier cakes, elaborate fondant figures (a full Elsa with gown detail, a Spiderman in mid-swing pose), or scene-based designs with multiple elements need 4–5 days minimum. These take significantly longer to build and require drying time for fondant pieces. Don't leave these for the last minute — we'd rather tell you early and get it right than rush and disappoint.

School Holidays — Order as Early as Possible

During NSW school holiday periods, our custom order queue fills up 2–3 weeks in advance. If your child's birthday falls during January, April, July, or October breaks, we strongly recommend reaching out the moment you confirm the party date. This is especially true for elaborate designs or for parties over a long weekend.

What Size Cake Do You Need for a Kids Party?

Sizing is something many parents underestimate. A 6-inch cake looks generous when it's just the family, but it disappears fast at a kids party with 20 guests. Here's a rough guide.

From our experience with party orders in the Marsden Park and Schofields area, the average kids birthday party runs bigger than parents expect. Families here tend to invite extended family alongside the child's school friends — so what starts as "about 15 kids" often becomes 30 people including parents and grandparents. We'd always recommend going one size up from what you initially calculated, especially if you're also serving other food and the cake is the centrepiece rather than the entire dessert.

  • 6-inch (round, 2-layer): serves approximately 8–12 slices. Good for intimate family celebrations or as a smash cake companion.
  • 8-inch (round, 2-layer): serves approximately 16–20 slices. The most popular size for standard birthday parties.
  • 10-inch (round, 2-layer): serves approximately 24–30 slices. Best for larger gatherings, extended family events, or parties where cake is the main dessert.
  • Two-tier (6+8 inch): serves approximately 30–40 slices and creates a visual centrepiece. Popular for milestone birthdays and parties with a strong theme.
  • Cupcake towers: an alternative to a main cake — 24 or 48 individually frosted cupcakes on a stand. Great for kids who like individual portions and takes the stress out of cutting.
