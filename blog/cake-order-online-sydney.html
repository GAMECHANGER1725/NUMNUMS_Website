What Cake Sizes Can You Order, and How Many Do They Serve?

As a guide, an 8-inch round serves around 20, a 10-inch around 35, a 12-inch around 50, and a two-tier cake 70 or more. From our internal ordering experience, most family birthdays land on an 8 or 10-inch, while larger gatherings and events do best with a 12-inch or multiple cakes in different flavours. Slice size shifts the count, so round up for a hungry crowd.

How Far in Advance Should You Order a Cake Online?

Order at least 48 hours ahead for a standard cake, and 4–5 days for anything custom or large. From our order history, the single most common reason an order can't be filled is simply too little notice — every cake is made fresh, not pulled from a fridge, so the lead time is real. Booking earlier also locks in your preferred flavour and pick-up slot.

Is It Safe to Order a Cake Online for Dietary Needs?

Ordering online is the easiest way to handle dietary needs, because you can spell them out in writing and we confirm them back to you. According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), egg and milk are among the priority allergens requiring strict labelling — and because our kitchen is entirely egg-free, the egg question is already settled before you ask.

A few honest points so you can order with confidence:

  • Eggless by default — every flavour suits egg-allergic, Hindu vegetarian and Jain guests with no special request.
  • Contains dairy — our cakes use milk products, so they're eggless but not vegan.
  • Nuts and gluten — some flavours involve nuts, and standard cakes contain wheat; flag these when you order.
  • Ask first — for any allergen beyond egg, message us before ordering for a full ingredient breakdown.
