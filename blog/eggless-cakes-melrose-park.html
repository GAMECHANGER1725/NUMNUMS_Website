Are Eggless Cakes Suitable for Melrose Park's Cultural and Dietary Needs?

According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), eggs are one of the eight priority food allergens in Australia, with strict labelling laws attached. For egg-allergic families, cross-contamination from a bakery that uses eggs in other products is a genuine risk — one that a dedicated egg-free kitchen removes entirely.

At Num Num's Bakery, eggs are not used in any product, any preparation area, or any equipment on-site. That distinction matters for a wide range of households around Melrose Park. It goes well beyond just one cultural group:

  • Hindu, Jain and Sikh vegetarian households. Many South Asian dietary traditions exclude eggs as a form of lacto-vegetarian practice.
  • Chinese and Korean households — the largest overseas-born communities in the combined Ermington-Melrose Park area. Many order cakes for birthdays and Lunar New Year without any egg-specific requirement, but still appreciate consistent texture and quality.
  • Halal-observant households — our entirely egg-free, single-purpose kitchen sidesteps most cross-contamination questions around shared equipment (see our dedicated guide to halal-friendly eggless cakes in Sydney).
  • Vegan or plant-based families — note our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter); we are not a vegan bakery, but we are fully egg-free.
