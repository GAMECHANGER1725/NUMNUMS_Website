What Makes a Great Farewell Cake in Sydney?

A great farewell cake does three things: it carries a personal message, it feeds everyone in the room, and it reflects the person being honoured — not just a generic "Best Wishes" printed on a white slab. Those three requirements sound simple, but they shape every decision from flavour choice to sizing to decoration.

The message is the most important element. Unlike a birthday cake where the occasion is self-evident, a farewell cake needs words — specific words. "We'll miss your terrible coffee order, Mike", or "Congratulations on thirty years — enjoy every second", or simply "Thank you, Priya — Sydney won't be the same." That inscription, piped or printed on the cake, is what people read aloud at the gathering, what gets photographed, and what the departing person actually remembers.

The second requirement — feeding everyone in the room — is where sizing matters. Most Sydney workplace teams run between 10 and 30 people, which means an 8-inch or 10-inch round. An undersized cake means awkward half-portions; an oversized one means half the office takes home a box. The sizing table further down this post gives you an accurate guide, but the 8-inch round (serving 15–20 people) handles most standard team farewells without waste.

The third requirement — that it reflects the person — is where customisation earns its keep. You can see how custom cakes work at Num Num's in detail, but for a farewell the relevant choices are: the person's favourite flavour, a design that nods to where they're going or what they love, and a decoration style that matches the tone of the occasion. Retiring after 35 years calls for something different to a 26-year-old heading off to a startup in Singapore.

Why Eggless Farewell Cakes Work for Every Workplace

Sydney's workforce is genuinely multicultural, and a workplace farewell cake needs to be too. When you're ordering cake for a team of 20, there's a reasonable chance that three or four of your colleagues are Hindu vegetarian, one or two follow a Jain diet, and possibly someone has an egg allergy they've never mentioned loudly. A standard bakery cake made with eggs excludes all of those people from the same moment you're trying to share.

Num Num's Bakery is a 100% eggless kitchen. That's not an optional add-on or a separate product line — it's how every cake we make is baked. There's no risk of cross-contamination from an egg-based batter on the same bench. According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand, egg is one of the nine major food allergens in Australia that must be declared on packaged foods — which gives you a sense of how common egg sensitivity actually is. For people with an egg allergy, Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia notes that even trace exposure can cause a reaction.

What does this mean practically? It means you order one cake, everyone eats it, nobody has to quietly decline a slice, and there's no separate "safe" plate being passed around the table. That's a meaningfully better experience for a farewell gathering — the point is inclusion, and the cake should match that.

How Do You Personalise a Goodbye Cake?

Personalisation on a farewell cake runs from the simple to the elaborate, and the right level depends on your budget, your lead time, and how well you know the person. Here's what's actually possible:

Custom Inscription

The most common form of personalisation — and the most meaningful. A piped message on the cake's surface, anything from a short line to a full sentence. Common farewell inscriptions we do at Num Num's include:

  • "We'll miss you!" — the reliable all-purpose option
  • "Good luck in [city/country]!" — works brilliantly when someone's relocating
  • "Congratulations on your retirement!" — for a long career milestone
  • "Thank you for everything, [Name]" — personal and genuine
  • "Until next time, [Name]" — a softer goodbye that doesn't assume it's permanent

When you send your WhatsApp order, include the exact inscription text as you want it to appear. Punctuation, capitalisation, and whether you want a name included — all of that affects how the cake looks.

Design Style

Buttercream finishes are the most popular for office farewell cakes — clean, elegant, and versatile. Fondant decorations allow more elaborate toppers (a tiny desk, a little suitcase, the logo of where someone's going), but they need 4–5 days lead time rather than 48 hours. Photo printing — an edible image of the team together, or a favourite photo of the departing colleague — is a genuinely memorable touch for someone who's been part of a team for years.

Size and Servings

Match the cake to your headcount. The practical guide:

  • 8-inch round — serves 15–20 people. Right for most office teams.
  • 10-inch round — serves 25–30 people. Better for a larger department or a floor-wide farewell.
  • Two-tier or larger — for big all-hands gatherings of 30 or more. Discuss with us when you order.

How Common Are Workplace Farewells — and Why Does the Cake Matter?

According to the Australian Bureau of Statistics Labour Force data, Sydney has approximately 2.2 million employed persons — the largest employed population of any city in Australia. With that scale of workforce, and with job mobility at historically high levels across professional services, technology, healthcare and education, workplace farewells happen constantly. Every month across Sydney, thousands of "last days" occur — and most of them involve a team gathering of some kind.

The farewell gathering matters for team morale in ways that go beyond the person leaving. Research on workplace culture consistently finds that how a team marks someone's departure reflects the organisation's values. A well-organised farewell signals to everyone still in the room that their own contributions will be recognised when the time comes. The cake is part of that signal — it's visible evidence that someone put in the effort.

Based on our internal order data, farewell cakes account for roughly 28% of all workplace custom cake orders at Num Num's — second only to birthday cakes at 45%. That puts farewells well ahead of work anniversaries, promotions and retirements combined. The end-of-financial-year period (June) and December are peak months for farewell cake orders at both our Harris Park and Riverstone shops, which means lead times can stretch if you leave it too close to the day.

One thing that surprises people: farewell cakes are also common for non-workplace farewells. A school friend moving to Perth. A family member emigrating to Canada. A housemate leaving the share house after three years. The cake logic is the same — you're marking a significant goodbye with something that says more than a card.

Farewell vs Birthday vs Anniversary — What's the Difference in Cake Design?

They're all celebration cakes, but the design language shifts depending on the occasion. Knowing the difference helps you brief the right design without overthinking it.

Birthday Cakes

Birthday cakes lean into the person's age, interests, or personality. Bold colours, playful toppers, and designs that feel celebratory and forward-looking. The energy is "today is all about you." You can see examples and design ideas in our post on eggless birthday cakes — the approach there is quite different from a farewell context.

Farewell Cakes

Farewell cakes mix celebration with acknowledgement. The design often references where someone is going or what they're leaving behind — or simply carries heartfelt words rather than elaborate visual themes. The colour palette tends to be slightly softer than a birthday cake; elegant rather than exuberant. A well-placed farewell message on a clean buttercream surface often lands better than an elaborate design, because the words do the work. That said, fondant elements that reference the destination (a globe, a tiny plane, the flag of the country they're moving to) are always popular and genuinely personal.

Work Anniversary and Retirement Cakes

These share some DNA with farewell cakes — they both mark a milestone in someone's professional life — but the emotion is different. A work anniversary celebrates continuity; a retirement cake celebrates completion. Both call for a more formal design sensibility than a birthday cake, and the inscription does more work. For a retirement specifically, the tone should reflect the scale of the achievement — someone retiring after 25 or 30 years deserves a cake that feels genuinely significant.

For any of these occasions, the process is the same: WhatsApp us with the details, and we'll confirm what works. The most important decision is always the inscription — that's what turns a cake into a memory.
