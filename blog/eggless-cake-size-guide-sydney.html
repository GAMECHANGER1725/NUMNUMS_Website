What Size Eggless Cake Do You Need? A Sydney Serving Guide

By Tarun Patel 4 June 2026 9 min read Eggless Cakes · Guide

Choosing a flavour is the fun part of ordering an eggless cake. Choosing the size is where people freeze. Order too small and you're cutting slivers while half the room misses out; order too big and you're sending cake home in takeaway containers. This guide gives you the simple numbers — how many people each eggless cake size really serves — so you can order with confidence for any Sydney celebration.

Here's the short answer up front: an 8-inch round eggless cake serves about 12–15 people as party slices, and a 10-inch serves roughly 20–25. Everything else — shape, tiers, kids versus adults — adjusts around those two anchor points. Below, we break it down size by size, with a table you can screenshot.

What Size Eggless Cake Do You Need?

Start with your guest count, then match it to a round size: that's the whole method. As a working rule, allow one generous party slice per adult and add a small buffer of 10–15% for second helpings and unexpected arrivals. From our order data across thousands of Sydney celebrations, the 8-inch and 10-inch sit at the centre of demand because they cover the two most common party sizes — a household birthday and a larger family gathering.

How Many People Does Each Cake Size Serve?

A 10-inch round eggless cake serves roughly 20–25 people as party slices — the single most useful number for planning a milestone birthday. From there, each step up or down in diameter changes the area, and therefore the serve count, more than people expect: a 10-inch holds nearly 60% more cake than an 8-inch, even though it sounds like a small jump.

That non-linear jump is the most common sizing mistake we see. Two inches of diameter is not "a little bigger" — it's a meaningful step in surface area. So if you're hovering between two sizes for a group that might grow, size up: the cost difference is modest and a slightly larger cake rescues a party far better than a slightly smaller one strands it.

And if you do end up with leftovers, an eggless cake keeps beautifully. Covered in the fridge, most of our flavours stay moist and good for three to four days, so a slightly generous order rarely goes to waste — it just becomes the next afternoon's treat.

The quick-reference numbers

  • 6-inch: ~6–8 party slices. Perfect for an intimate dinner or a small family birthday.
  • 8-inch: ~12–15 party slices. Our most-ordered size and the safe default for a typical birthday.
  • 10-inch: ~20–25 party slices. The go-to for milestone birthdays and larger family gatherings.
  • 12-inch: ~30–40 party slices. A genuine event cake for big crowds.
  • Two-tier: ~35–60 depending on tier sizes, and it lets you offer two flavours at once.

Round, Sheet or Tiered: Which Shape Suits Your Event?

Shape changes both the serve count and the feel of the cake, so pick it to match the event, not just the headcount. A round cake is the celebratory centrepiece most people picture; a tiered cake scales that drama up for weddings and big milestones; and for very large, casual crowds a larger or two-tier format is simply easier to portion. From our experience, the right call is usually about how the cake will be cut and served, not only how many mouths there are.

Here's a pattern worth knowing: for 40-plus guests, a two-tier cake almost always beats one oversized round. It carries more height and presence on the table, it's easier for one person to cut into even slices, and — the part people miss — it lets you split flavours. A Chocolate base with a Vanilla or Rasmalai top means a mixed crowd gets a choice, and you avoid the "I wish we'd got chocolate" sigh halfway through the afternoon.

Tiered cakes do need more lead time and a sturdier transport plan, so order a few days ahead and bring a flat boot space for pick-up. If you'd rather keep it simple, a single 12-inch round still comfortably feeds a large party — it just asks for a confident hand on the knife.

What about a sheet or slab cake? A rectangular cake is the most efficient shape for sheer numbers — there's no rounded edge wasting tray space, so it portions into neat, uniform squares with very little offcut. For an office farewell, a school fete or any event where serving speed matters more than a showpiece centrepiece, a slab is the practical choice. The trade-off is presence: a slab reads as casual and generous rather than special, so for milestone moments most of our customers still prefer a round or tiered cake and accept the slightly lower yield. Tell us the occasion and we'll steer you to the shape that fits.

How Do Kids' Portions Change the Size You Need?

At a children's party you can usually drop a size, because kids eat smaller slices. From our order history, an 8-inch eggless cake often stretches to 18–20 children where it would serve about 14 adults — children rarely finish a full adult slice, and many are more excited by the candles than the cake itself.

The catch we flag for every parent: count the adults too. At most kids' parties, the mums, dads, aunties and grandparents are standing right there, and they eat adult-sized slices. From our orders, the families who run short are almost always the ones who sized for "20 kids" and forgot the 15 adults hovering by the table. If grown-ups are staying, size for the total head-count, not just the children.

For very young toddlers' parties, a 6-inch "smash cake" plus a larger sheet or round for the adults is a popular combination — the little one gets their own cake for the photos, and everyone else gets a proper slice. We're happy to make both in matching flavours.

Does a Custom Design Need a Bigger Eggless Cake?

Sometimes, yes — an elaborate design can push you up a size for structural reasons, not just servings. Tall buttercream cakes, sculpted shapes and heavily decorated tiers need enough cake underneath to carry the design, so a detailed brief occasionally lands on a 10-inch where the headcount alone suggested an 8-inch. We'll tell you upfront if a design needs the extra base.

The reverse is true for simple, elegant cakes: a clean buttercream finish on the right size delivers exactly the servings in the table with no compromise. If your budget and headcount are fixed, tell us both and we'll design to fit — there's always a way to make a smaller cake look generous. Why Choose a 100% Eggless Cake for Your Celebration?

Because an eggless cake includes everyone at the table — and demand for it is rising sharply. As of August 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report found that 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — now live with allergic disease, up from 4.1 million (19.6%) in 2007. Egg is one of the most common childhood food allergens, and an egg-free cake quietly removes that worry from the dessert.

It's not only about allergy. Many lacto-vegetarian Hindu and Jain households exclude eggs entirely, so an eggless cake means the whole family shares the same slice without anyone opting out. At Num Num's, eggless isn't a substitution — every cake, in every size above, is 100% eggless and made in an egg-free kitchen. One honest note: eggless does not mean allergen-free. Our cakes contain dairy and may involve nuts, and standard cakes contain wheat — see Food Standards Australia New Zealand for the full allergen list, and tell us about any severe allergy when you order.
