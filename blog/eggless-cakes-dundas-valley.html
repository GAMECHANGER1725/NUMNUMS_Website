Why Is Eggless Cake So Hard to Find Near Dundas Valley?

In 2025, allergic disease affected 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population, up from 4.1 million (19.6%) in 2007, according to the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report (NACE/Deloitte, August 2025). That's a doubling of allergic disease prevalence in under two decades. Among the most common food triggers is egg: according to Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia, egg allergy affects approximately 9% of Australian infants, making it one of the most prevalent food allergies in the country.

Most general bakeries don't invest in that level of reformulation. Num Num's Bakery did, and it's the only thing we make.

Egg-free baking aligns with several dietary frameworks common across the City of Parramatta LGA. For families managing egg allergy — particularly households with young children, where egg allergy affects roughly 9% of Australian infants — ordering from a dedicated egg-free kitchen removes the cross-contamination risk that a mixed bakery cannot fully eliminate.

At Num Num's Bakery, the kitchen does not use eggs in any product, at any preparation stage, on any equipment. This isn't a recipe adjustment — it's the entire operational model. That matters enormously for allergy-affected families, and it also matters for a range of cultural and religious dietary frameworks that are well represented in the Dundas Valley catchment:

  • Hindu vegetarian households — many Hindu dietary traditions, particularly lacto-vegetarian practice, exclude eggs. For these households, a bakery where every cake is egg-free by default is not a convenience but a baseline requirement
  • Sikh families — some Sikh dietary codes exclude eggs, and the Parramatta–Carlingford corridor has a significant Sikh community for whom egg-free options are a dietary standard
