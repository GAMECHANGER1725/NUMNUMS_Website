Why Is It Hard to Find Eggless Cakes Near Wentworth Point?

Egg allergy affects roughly 9% of Australian infants, according to Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia, and egg is one of the eight priority food allergens regulated under Australian food law. Most bakeries around the Wentworth Point and Sydney Olympic Park corridor still treat an eggless request as a one-off swap rather than a genuine reformulation. The difference usually shows the moment you cut into the cake.

Reformulating a recipe without eggs — not as a quick substitution, but from the ground up — takes real trial and error. Eggs bind, aerate and add moisture all at once. Replace them poorly and the result is a denser crumb, a flatter rise and a cake that dries out within a day. Few general bakeries invest years of testing for what they see as a niche order. Num Num's Bakery did exactly that, and it's the entire menu, not a side option.

According to the Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) allergen guidance, egg is one of only eight foods that must be declared on Australian food labels regardless of quantity used. For a bakery, that makes a genuinely egg-free kitchen a meaningfully different proposition to a kitchen that simply leaves an ingredient out on request.

Are Eggless Cakes Suitable for Dietary and Cultural Needs Near Wentworth Point?

Egg is one of the eight priority allergens under Australian food law, and cross-contamination risk is real in any kitchen that also handles egg-based products, according to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ). At Num Num's Bakery, eggs are never used in any product, preparation surface or piece of equipment on-site — the entire kitchen is egg-free, not just the recipes.

That distinction matters for several groups likely to be reading this from Wentworth Point or a nearby suburb:

  • Families managing egg allergy or intolerance — a fully egg-free kitchen removes cross-contamination risk that a shared kitchen can't guarantee, which matters especially for toddler birthday parties.
  • Hindu vegetarian and Jain households — lacto-vegetarian dietary practice excludes eggs while still permitting dairy, which our menu fits by default.
  • Halal-observant households — an entirely egg-free kitchen sidesteps a category of cross-contamination question some mixed bakeries can't easily answer.
  • Vegan or plant-based families — note that our cakes still contain dairy (milk powder, butter); we're egg-free, not vegan, so check this before ordering if it matters to you.

For a severe or anaphylactic egg allergy, please tell us when you order. Follow your own medical or ASCIA-aligned advice. We take every precaution in our kitchen, but we can't promise medical-grade certification — your treating doctor's guidance should always take priority. If you have questions about other allergens beyond eggs, message us on WhatsApp before ordering and we'll give you a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour.
