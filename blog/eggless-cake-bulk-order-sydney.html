What Is a Bulk Eggless Cake Order — and Who Needs One?

A bulk eggless cake order is any booking that involves multiple cakes — typically 3 or more — for the same event or the same pick-up window. According to ABS Cafes, Restaurants and Catering Services data (2022–23), the catering segment of Australia's hospitality industry employed 178,700 people and generated substantial revenue, reflecting the scale of corporate and community event feeding in Australia. Within that market, dietary inclusivity — and specifically egg-free baking — has become a genuine planning priority rather than a niche accommodation.

So who actually places bulk eggless cake orders? In our experience across Harris Park and Riverstone, the customers who most frequently contact us for multi-cake orders fall into a handful of clear categories. Corporate HR and events teams are the largest single cohort — ordering cakes for team morning teas, milestone birthdays, work anniversaries, onboarding celebrations, and end-of-year parties where dietary inclusivity matters for a diverse workforce. Community and cultural organisations form the second-largest group: Diwali committees, temple groups, multicultural community centres, and cultural festival organisers who need to serve audiences that observe Hindu vegetarian or other egg-free dietary practices. Wedding and event planners represent a third category, particularly for South Asian and multicultural weddings where a 100% eggless guarantee is essential rather than optional.

Why Does Eggless Matter When Ordering Cakes in Bulk?

Egg allergy is one of the most common food allergies in Australia. According to Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia, egg allergy affects approximately 9% of Australian infants — making it the second most common food allergy in early childhood after peanut allergy. While many children outgrow egg allergy by school age, a significant proportion carry it into adulthood, and reactions can range from mild hives to anaphylaxis. When you're serving a group of 30, 50, or 100 guests, having even one person with a severe egg allergy creates a real catering challenge — unless your cake supplier uses no eggs at all.

But dietary necessity is only part of the story. Sydney's multicultural communities account for an enormous share of event catering demand, and for many of those communities, egg-free eating is not an allergy accommodation — it's a cultural norm. Hindu vegetarian dietary practice (lacto-vegetarianism) excludes eggs as a matter of principle, not medical necessity. Many Sikh households follow similar guidelines. Jain dietary practice excludes eggs entirely. When you're catering for a diverse workforce, a multicultural community event, or a South Asian wedding reception, ordering from a fully egg-free bakery removes the need for awkward dietary filtering entirely: every cake is safe for every guest who avoids eggs, for any reason.

The Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) classifies eggs as one of the eight priority allergens requiring mandatory declaration on food labels. At Num Num's Bakery, eggs are not present in any recipe, any ingredient, or any production environment at either of our locations. That means our bulk orders carry no hidden egg cross-contamination risk — which matters enormously when you're responsible for the dietary safety of a large group.

What Occasions Require Bulk Eggless Cake Orders in Sydney?

Based on our order history across Harris Park and Riverstone, these are the occasions that most frequently generate bulk eggless cake requests in Sydney:

Sydney's corporate sector is one of the city's most culturally diverse employment environments. When an HR team orders morning tea cakes for 60 employees, ordering eggless across the board means no one gets a different slice, no one has to ask awkward questions, and no one feels singled out. This is increasingly the default approach for inclusive office catering — and it's why eggless cakes for corporate events in Sydney have grown significantly as a category.

Diwali is Sydney's fastest-growing cultural festival. The Hindu community in Greater Sydney numbers in the hundreds of thousands, concentrated across Parramatta, Harris Park, Blacktown, and the Hills District. Schools in western and south-western Sydney often serve communities where a significant proportion of families have dietary restrictions. Fundraising events, fetes, and end-of-term celebrations that include egg-free cakes remove complexity for school canteen managers and event organisers. We supply multiple schools across the Parramatta and Blacktown LGAs for annual events.

Community centres, cultural associations, and religious organisations across Sydney regularly host multi-hundred-guest events where eggless catering is the only practical option. Whether it's a Navratri celebration, an Eid community meal, a Vaisakhi gathering, or a multicultural community fair, a fully eggless cake range removes the need for separate "dietary" options and simplifies the catering plan significantly.

What Flavours Work Best for a Bulk Cake Order?

Choosing flavours for a bulk eggless cake order is genuinely one of the more interesting planning decisions for any event. The right combination depends on your audience, the occasion, and how many cakes you're ordering. Here's how we think about it from years of handling bulk orders across both locations.

The Universal Crowd-Pleasers

Chocolate is our single most-ordered flavour, full stop — it transcends age, culture, and personal preference in a way no other flavour does. Vanilla is the second safest bet: it's neutral, universally accessible, and pairs well with any occasion. If you're uncertain about your group's preferences and you're ordering 5 or more cakes, always include at least one Chocolate and one Vanilla in the mix.

For South Asian and Multicultural Events

Rasmalai is consistently our top-performing flavour for Diwali, Navratri, Eid, and community cultural events. It's fragrant, delicately spiced, and immediately recognisable to guests from Indian and Pakistani backgrounds. Mango performs strongly in summer months and at events where a fresh, light option is welcome alongside richer choices. Butterscotch has broad cross-cultural appeal and is particularly popular among older guests.

For Corporate and Office Events

Corporate orders tend to favour a blend of accessible and premium: Chocolate and Vanilla for broad appeal, supplemented by Ferrero Rocher or Tiramisu for guests who want something more sophisticated. Cookies & Cream has become a reliable corporate favourite — it photographs well, reads as fun rather than serious, and pleases audiences across a wide age range.

For Weddings and Special Occasions

Wedding bulk orders most commonly feature Red Velvet (visually striking, subtly rich), Black Forest or White Forest (elegant, celebratory, and deeply satisfying), and Ferrero Rocher for the centrepiece cake. Couples ordering for a South Asian wedding reception frequently add Rasmalai and Mango to the mix for guests who prefer traditional flavour profiles.

How Many Cakes Do You Need for Your Event?

Sizing is the most common source of under-ordering for bulk cake events. People consistently underestimate how much cake a group will consume at a celebration — especially when the cake is the centrepiece rather than a dessert course. Here is our guide, based on standard 3-layer round cakes served as dessert-sized slices at events:

Cake Size Servings (event slices) Best for
6 inch round 10–12 slices Small team morning tea, intimate family gathering
8 inch round 20–24 slices Office team of 20, birthday party, baby shower
10 inch round 35–40 slices Department event, community dinner, smaller wedding
12 inch round 50–60 slices Large office event, Diwali celebration, school fundraiser
Tiered 2-layer 80–100 slices Wedding reception, large cultural event, corporate gala

A practical rule of thumb for bulk orders: plan for your guest count divided by the serving count of your chosen cake size, then round up. If you're hosting 80 guests and want to offer 2 flavour options, order two 10-inch cakes (serving 35–40 each) or one 12-inch and one 10-inch. For very large events of 150+ guests, a combination of tiered cakes and supporting round cakes gives you the visual impact of a centrepiece with the flexibility of multiple flavours. How Much Notice Do You Need for a Bulk Eggless Cake Order?

Notice periods are the single most common planning mistake for bulk eggless cake orders. Every cake at Num Num's Bakery is made fresh to order — we don't maintain a pre-made stock that bulk orders draw from. This is what guarantees freshness, but it also means that adequate lead time is essential, especially for larger orders.

Standard Single-Cake Orders

For a single cake in a standard size and flavour, 48 hours notice is our baseline minimum. This gives our bakers time to prepare, bake, and decorate your cake to the standard we hold for every order.

Bulk Orders of 3–5 Cakes

For orders of 3 to 5 cakes — the most common size for team events, baby showers, and small cultural celebrations — we recommend 4–5 days notice. This allows time to confirm the order in full, source any specific decoration elements, and schedule the baking across our production capacity at both locations.

Bulk Orders of 6–20 Cakes

For orders involving 6 or more cakes — corporate events, large Diwali celebrations, wedding receptions — we recommend a minimum of 7 days notice, and ideally 10–14 days for orders above 10 cakes. This isn't bureaucracy: it's the reality of fresh-made production at the volume required to serve your event well.

Peak Periods — Plan Further Ahead

Certain periods of the year create extremely high demand across both our locations. During these windows, we strongly recommend giving as much advance notice as possible — a minimum of 10–14 days, and 3–4 weeks where you know your event date well in advance:

  • Diwali (October/November) — our single busiest period of the year, with bulk orders often filling our capacity weeks in advance
  • Christmas and New Year (December) — office end-of-year parties and family celebrations create sustained high demand throughout the month
  • NSW School Holidays — kids' birthday parties cluster during school holiday periods; book early to secure your preferred dates
  • Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha — significant demand from multicultural communities across western Sydney
  • Valentine's Day, Mother's Day — peak romantic and family celebration periods

The safest approach: message us on WhatsApp as soon as your event date is confirmed, even if you're still deciding on flavours and quantities. We can place a provisional hold on your date while you finalise the details.

Can You Mix Flavours in a Bulk Order?

Yes — and this is one of the most popular aspects of placing a bulk order with Num Num's Bakery. You are not locked into a single flavour just because you're ordering multiple cakes. You can order 8 cakes with 8 different flavours, or you can order 8 cakes with 3 flavours in whatever split ratio suits your event. We'll confirm each flavour separately in the order summary.

From our order data, the most common flavour mix patterns for bulk orders are:

  • Corporate morning tea (5 cakes): 2 Chocolate + 1 Vanilla + 1 Ferrero Rocher + 1 Cookies & Cream
  • Diwali celebration (8 cakes): 3 Rasmalai + 2 Mango + 2 Chocolate + 1 Butterscotch
  • Wedding reception (10 cakes): 2 Red Velvet + 2 Black Forest + 2 Ferrero Rocher + 2 Rasmalai + 1 Vanilla + 1 Tiramisu
  • Baby shower (3 cakes): 1 Strawberry + 1 White Forest + 1 Vanilla
  • School fundraiser (6 cakes): 2 Chocolate + 2 Butterscotch + 1 Strawberry + 1 Blueberry

When communicating your flavour mix, include the full list in your WhatsApp message — flavour name and quantity for each. We'll confirm availability and flag any substitutions well in advance of your event date.

One practical tip: for events where you're displaying cakes side by side, consider varying the visual profile as well as the flavour. A red-tinted Red Velvet cake beside a cream-finished White Forest beside a rich dark Chocolate creates a naturally striking dessert table without any additional decoration investment. Our team can advise on visual pairing as part of the order conversation if you'd like guidance.
