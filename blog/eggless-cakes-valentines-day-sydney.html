Why Choose an Eggless Cake for Valentine's Day?

Because a romantic gesture shouldn't leave anyone out — and a surprising number of people can't eat egg-based cake. As of August 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report found 8.2 million Australians (30%) live with allergic disease, double the 2007 figure. With egg allergy affecting around 9% of infants per Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia, an eggless cake quietly removes a worry from the evening.

There's a romantic logic to it, too. In Sydney's many intercultural relationships, one partner may keep a Hindu vegetarian or Jain diet that excludes eggs, while the other doesn't think twice about it. An eggless cake is the one dessert that works for both — no separate "safe" option, no awkward explanation at the table. It's inclusivity that reads as thoughtfulness, which is exactly the tone Valentine's Day is going for.

And because eggless is our entire menu rather than a substitution, you're never trading taste for safety. The Red Velvet you order for your partner is the same celebrated recipe everyone else is eating — just made without eggs from the start.

It also takes the pressure off the planning. When you order from a bakery where eggless is the default, you don't have to vet a substitution, chase an ingredient list, or hope the "egg-free option" tastes as good as the real thing. The whole menu already qualifies, so you can focus on the design and the date — not on whether the cake is safe for the person you're celebrating.

How Do You Keep a Valentine's Cake Fresh Until the Big Night?

Store it by its finish, not its date. A fresh-cream Valentine's cake should stay in the fridge until about 20–30 minutes before you cut it, so the sponge loses its chill and tastes its softest. A fondant heart cake prefers a steady, cool room temperature on the day — fondant can sweat if it jumps from a cold fridge into a warm room.

Because every Num Num's cake is made fresh to order rather than baked ahead, collecting on the 13th or the morning of the 14th keeps it at its best for the evening. Keep it boxed and out of direct sun on the drive home, and give a cream cake a short rest out of the fridge before serving. For the full routine, see our eggless cake storage and freshness guide.

Are Eggless Valentine's Cakes Suitable for Dietary and Cultural Needs?

Yes — that's the point of an all-eggless kitchen. According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), egg is a priority allergen that must be declared on labels. Because we never use eggs in any product or on any equipment, there's no egg cross-contamination pathway — which suits Hindu vegetarian, Jain and Sikh partners, and egg-allergy households, on a night when you'd rather not be reading ingredient lists.

One honest note: egg-free is not allergen-free. Our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter), standard cakes contain wheat, and some recipes may involve nuts. We're not a vegan bakery. For a severe or anaphylactic egg allergy, tell us your exact needs when ordering and follow your own medical or ASCIA-aligned advice — we'll give you a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen cake.
