Can You Really Get a Same Day Cake in Sydney?

According to the Australian Bureau of Statistics' hospitality industry data, consumer expectations around on-demand food service have shifted dramatically since 2020. Same-day delivery has become standard across much of the food sector, and many Sydneysiders now assume the same applies to custom cakes. It doesn't — and understanding why actually helps you get a better cake.

The short answer: some places will say yes to same day. But "same day" at most bakeries means one of two things: either they're selling you something pre-baked and sitting in a display case, or they're rushing a job that deserves more time. Neither of those outcomes serves you at a moment that matters.

Quality bakeries — the ones producing genuinely fresh, made-to-order cakes with consistent results — typically require 24 to 48 hours at minimum. The best among them, including Num Num's, build that lead time into their process because freshness is the product. A cake that was baked this morning for a party tonight is a fundamentally different thing from a cake that was baked yesterday morning and sat in refrigeration since. You can taste the difference, and so can every guest at the table.

That said, if you are in a genuine same-day bind right now — message us on WhatsApp. We'll check both our Harris Park and Riverstone locations and be honest about what's possible. We'd rather give you a real answer than have you go elsewhere and be disappointed.

Why Do Quality Cakes Need at Least 48 Hours Notice?

The 48-hour window isn't arbitrary. It reflects the actual timeline of what needs to happen between placing an order and producing a cake worth celebrating with. Here's what that process looks like at Num Num's:

  1. Order confirmation and ingredient staging — we confirm your flavour, size, any custom design brief, and schedule your bake slot within our daily production calendar
  2. Baking — eggless cakes require precise chemistry. Without eggs as a binding and leavening agent, we rely on carefully calibrated combinations of buttermilk, baking powder, and other agents. Rushing this produces a denser, less consistent result
  3. Cooling — a freshly baked cake must cool completely before it can be layered or decorated. This typically takes 2–4 hours. Attempting to layer or frost a warm cake causes the cream to melt and slide — the result is visually and texturally compromised
  4. Layering and filling — each layer of cream, fruit, ganache, or flavoured filling is applied by hand and allowed to set before the next is added
  5. Crumb coating and final decoration — the outer layer, any fondant work, piping, or custom design elements are applied last, often over multiple stages

From a food safety perspective, this matters too. Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) recommends that fresh cream cakes be consumed within 2–3 days of preparation and kept refrigerated at all times below 5°C. Our 48-hour process ensures your cake is baked as close to your event date as possible — maximising freshness rather than minimising it.

Eggless baking adds another layer of consideration. As outlined by NSW Food Authority guidance on food business safety programs, specialty bakeries working without standard egg-based binding must follow rigorous process controls to achieve consistent food safety outcomes. Our 15 eggless flavours each have their own baking profile. Chocolate behaves differently to Rasmalai; Ferrero Rocher requires different assembly time to Strawberry. The 48-hour window gives us the time to do each of them properly.

What Happens When You Order a Same Day Cake vs. a Pre-Ordered Cake?

The difference between a shelf cake and a made-to-order cake is significant — and most people who've experienced both know it immediately. Here's what actually separates them:

Shelf cakes are typically baked in bulk batches, often the night before or sometimes multiple days ahead. They're designed to have a longer display life, which usually means more preservatives, more sugar (which extends shelf life), and a denser, more forgiving crumb structure. The cream layers may have been applied hours ago and have begun to separate or weep. The sponge may have absorbed some moisture from the cream and softened unevenly. These aren't necessarily bad cakes — but they're optimised for display longevity, not peak freshness.

Made-to-order cakes — like every cake at Num Num's — are baked specifically for your event and collected within 24 hours of completion. The sponge is at peak moisture. The cream hasn't had time to weep. The flavours are most vibrant within the first 24 hours after assembly. If you've ever cut into a cake and noticed the layers look perfectly even and the cream holds its shape cleanly, you're looking at something that was made recently and handled well.

The gap matters even more for eggless cakes. An eggless sponge that's been sitting for 48+ hours before you even collect it can feel noticeably denser than one collected fresh. The 48-hour advance order process at Num Num's exists precisely to make sure that the cake you collect was baked for you, not made on the off-chance someone would buy it.

For custom cakes in Sydney, this distinction is even more pronounced. Custom designs — whether that's a specific colour scheme, a character, a photo transfer, or a particular flavour combination — simply cannot be done well in a few hours. The 48-hour window is the minimum for a standard design; complex or tiered cakes need more.
