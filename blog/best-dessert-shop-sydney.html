The Best Dessert Shop in Western Sydney — What to Expect & Where to Go

15 May 2026 8 min read Desserts · Sydney

The phrase "best dessert shop" gets searched thousands of times a month across Sydney. But what people actually mean when they type it varies: some want a walk-in café with display cases and coffee. Some want a shop that does proper custom celebration cakes. Some have dietary requirements — no eggs, halal, vegetarian — and want a dessert shop that handles that without making it a big deal.

This article is specifically for people in western Sydney who fit that last description. We'll cover what makes a dessert shop genuinely worth visiting, why Num Num's Bakery stands out in this region, what's on the menu, and where to find us.

What Actually Makes a Dessert Shop the Best?

Before we talk about Num Num's specifically, it's worth being clear about what the bar should be when you're looking for the best dessert shop in your area. There are plenty of options in Sydney — from chain bakeries at Westfield to Instagram-friendly dessert bars in the CBD. But "best" usually means something specific to the person searching.

Here are the criteria that matter most:

Freshness
The best dessert shops don't rely on sitting product. A cake that's been in a display case since Tuesday is not the same as one made for you yesterday. The difference in texture, moisture, and flavour is significant — especially for layered custom cakes.
Craft and Consistency
A shop that nails one flavour once isn't remarkable. A shop that delivers the same quality across 15 different flavours, every order, every week — that's craft. Consistency is harder to achieve than novelty.
Dietary Inclusivity
In Sydney's west, a dessert shop that can serve every person at the table — regardless of whether they avoid eggs, follow a vegetarian or Jain diet, or have halal requirements — is meaningfully better than one that can't. This is not a niche; it's the majority of customers in suburbs like Harris Park, Parramatta, and the north-west corridor.
Custom Work
The best dessert shops can make what you have in mind — not just what's already on the shelf. A proper custom cake for a 30th birthday, a baby shower, a Diwali celebration, or an Eid gathering requires both skill and a willingness to engage with the customer's brief.
Accessible Hours and Location
A brilliant dessert shop that's only open on weekday afternoons, or only accessible by a 40-minute drive, doesn't serve most people. The best dessert shops are genuinely local — there when you need them.

Why Num Num's Bakery Is Western Sydney's Best Dessert Shop

Num Num's Bakery was founded with a specific purpose: to be the dessert shop that western Sydney's diverse community actually needed. Not an eggless option offered reluctantly alongside egg-containing products, but a bakery where 100% eggless is the only way things are done. Every cake, every cupcake, every product that leaves either location has never contained eggs.

That's the foundation. But it's the execution on top of that foundation — the flavour quality, the custom design capability, the freshness of every order — that makes it the best dessert shop in this part of Sydney.

Every Product Is Made Fresh to Order

There is no display case of cakes that have been sitting since yesterday at Num Num's. Every custom order is made fresh for the customer who placed it, prepared within the window before their pick-up time. This is not standard practice across most bakeries — it requires more planning from the customer (you need to order ahead) but the payoff in quality is unmistakable. Moist crumb, vivid flavour, correct structure. These are the hallmarks of a cake made today, not carried over from last week.

The Eggless Standard Is Non-Negotiable

At most bakeries, "eggless" is an accommodation — a version of the standard recipe with something swapped in. The result is often a cake that's clearly different: denser, slightly gummy, lacking the rise and texture of the egg-containing version. Num Num's doesn't operate this way. The recipes were developed from scratch to be 100% eggless — they're not compromises. First-time customers consistently remark that they couldn't tell the difference.

For families in western Sydney where dietary restrictions around eggs are common — whether for religious, ethical, or health reasons — this isn't just convenient. It's the difference between a dessert shop that works for your household and one that doesn't.

What You Can Order

Custom Celebration Cakes

This is the heart of what Num Num's does. Custom cakes for birthdays, baby showers, bridal showers, weddings, naming ceremonies, graduations, Diwali, Eid, Christmas — any occasion where you want a centrepiece dessert that was made specifically for your event. Sizes range from a 6-inch cake for an intimate gathering through to multi-tiered showpieces for 50 or more guests.

The design process is simple: message your brief on WhatsApp (theme, colours, any text, flavour preference), and the team will work through what's possible. For intricate fondant work, sugar flowers, or complex tiered structures, give 4–5 days notice. For standard custom designs, 48 hours is sufficient.

Cupcakes

Custom cupcake orders are available for parties, corporate events, and gifting. Cupcakes can be ordered in the same 15 flavours as the cakes, with custom frosting colours and toppers. They're a popular choice for children's birthday parties — easier to serve, no cutting required, and every child gets their own portion.

Occasion-Specific Dessert Orders

Beyond the standard menu, Num Num's takes occasion briefs seriously. A Diwali order might call for specific colour themes and decorative motifs. A wedding dessert table might require a tiered cake plus matching cupcakes in the same flavour palette. A baby shower might need a pastel colour scheme with a custom gender-reveal interior. These requests are handled regularly — the team has experience across all of these.

The Two Locations — Both Worth Knowing

Harris Park — The Original Location

The Harris Park bakery at 96/96 Wigram Street, Harris Park NSW 2150 is the flagship. It sits on the main strip of the Wigram Street precinct — one of Sydney's most vibrant South Asian food and retail destinations. It's open every single day from 11 am to 10 pm, including weekends and public holidays, making it one of the most accessible dessert shops in the greater Parramatta area.

From Parramatta, Westmead, Merrylands, Granville, Auburn, and surrounding suburbs, Harris Park is your closest Num Num's location. It's walkable from Harris Park and Parramatta train stations.

Riverstone — The North-West Location

The Riverstone bakery at Shop 8, Riverstone Shopping Centre, Riverstone NSW 2765 serves the rapidly growing north-western corridor. Open Monday to Friday from 6 am to 8 pm and Saturday and Sunday from 7 am to 7 pm, it's the closest dessert shop for residents of Marsden Park, Talawong, Schofields, The Ponds, Box Hill, Rouse Hill, and Stanhope Gardens.

The early weekday opening at Riverstone is particularly useful for customers picking up on the way to work, or collecting before a morning event.
