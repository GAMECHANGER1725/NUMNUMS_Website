Eggless Cake Catering in Sydney: Bulk Orders for Every Event

By Tarun Patel 19 June 2026 9 min read Catering · Eggless Cakes

Planning a Sydney event where multiple guests avoid eggs — whether for Hindu vegetarian, Sikh, Jain, egg allergy, or personal dietary reasons — presents a genuine catering challenge. A single conventional cake can exclude a significant portion of the room before the first slice is cut. And asking the bakery for an "eggless version" too often produces a denser, drier result that everyone notices. Eggless cake catering solves this at the source, making the dessert genuinely inclusive for every guest, without a separate option, without a visible difference, and without awkward explanations at the dessert table.

How Many Cakes Do I Need for My Sydney Event?

Cake sizing for events follows a practical rule of thumb: allow one standard serve (roughly 100–120g) per guest. From our catering experience across Sydney events, an 8-inch round cake yields approximately 10–15 serves, a 10-inch yields 18–25, and a 12-inch yields 30–40. For events of 50 or more guests, most hosts order two or more cakes of different flavours — creating a cake table that allows guests to choose, provides visual variety, and removes the anxiety of running short mid-event.

For events of 100 or more guests, many of our customers order 3 or 4 cakes of different flavours and present them on a dessert table. From our catering history, this approach consistently draws more engagement than a single large cake — guests move around the table, try multiple flavours, and the dessert becomes a talking point rather than a formality. It also allows the host to include both a crowd-pleasing flavour (Chocolate or Butterscotch) alongside a culturally resonant choice (Rasmalai or Mango) without having to pick just one.

Is Eggless Cake Catering Inclusive for All Dietary Needs at Sydney Events?

According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), eggs are one of the eight priority food allergens in Australia that require mandatory declaration on food packaging and menus. For event hosts, the practical implication is clear: ordering from a 100% eggless bakery eliminates the egg allergen from the dessert item entirely — not just from the recipe, but from the entire kitchen environment.

Num Num's Bakery is a 100% egg-free kitchen. Eggs are not used in any product, any preparation area, or any equipment on-site. This isn't just a recipe-level decision — it's a kitchen-level commitment that matters for families managing genuine egg allergy.

Important allergen honesty: Num Num's cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter) and may involve nuts. Num Num's Bakery is not a vegan or dairy-free bakery. For guests with specific allergen needs beyond eggs — including dairy intolerance, nut allergy, or severe anaphylactic egg allergy — please contact us directly on WhatsApp before placing your order. We will provide a complete ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour. For guidance on managing severe food allergies at events, ASCIA (allergy.org.au) and Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia provide comprehensive resources for hosts and caterers.

With those caveats in place, Num Num's 100% eggless kitchen directly serves the following communities at Sydney events:

  • Hindu vegetarian (lacto-vegetarian) households — many Hindu dietary traditions exclude eggs as a non-vegetarian product. Our entire menu is eggless by default, not by substitution.
  • Sikh guests — some Sikh dietary codes exclude eggs. No special request needed at Num Num's; the standard menu is already egg-free.
  • Jain households (on the egg axis) — Jain dietary practice excludes eggs. Our full range is Jain-compatible from an egg perspective.
  • Egg-allergic guests — the whole-kitchen egg-free environment reduces cross-contamination risk significantly. For anaphylactic egg allergy, confirm kitchen practices directly before ordering.
  • Personal dietary preference — a growing number of guests avoid eggs by choice, independent of religious or medical reasons. Eggless catering covers them without requiring anyone to self-identify.
