Is Num Num's Bakery Safe for Egg Allergies and Dietary Needs in Harris Park?

Eggs are never used anywhere in the Harris Park kitchen — not in a recipe, a preparation surface, or a piece of equipment — which removes the cross-contamination pathway that egg-allergic families need to worry about at a mixed bakery. According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), eggs are one of the eight priority food allergens requiring strict labelling under Australian food law, so an entirely egg-free kitchen is a meaningful distinction, not a marketing claim.

That said, being egg-free is not the same as being allergen-free. Our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter) and standard flavours contain wheat; some designs involve nuts. If you or a family member has a severe or anaphylactic allergy, tell us when you order and follow your own medical advice — for authoritative guidance, see ASCIA and Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia. We'll give you a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour on request; we can't promise medical-grade safety beyond the egg-free kitchen itself.

Being fully egg-free also lines up with several dietary frameworks common in Harris Park's community:

  • Hindu vegetarian households — many Hindu dietary traditions treat eggs as non-vegetarian (lacto-vegetarian practice); Hinduism is the leading religious affiliation in Harris Park at 46.9%
  • Jain households — Jain dietary practices exclude eggs entirely; our whole menu is Jain-compatible on the egg axis
  • Sikh families — some Sikh dietary codes exclude eggs, and Punjabi is spoken at home by 6.4% of Harris Park residents
  • Halal-observant households — eggs from conventional sources are considered halal, but cross-contamination with non-halal meats in a mixed bakery can be a concern; an entirely egg-free kitchen sidesteps this question
  • Egg allergy sufferers — the egg-free kitchen removes the egg cross-contamination risk specifically; other allergens (dairy, wheat, nuts) still apply and should be discussed with us directly
