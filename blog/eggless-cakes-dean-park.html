Are Eggless Cakes Suitable for Dean Park's Multicultural Families?

Eggs are one of the eight priority food allergens regulated under Australian law, according to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ). In 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence found that 30% of Australians — 8.2 million people — live with allergic disease, up from 19.6% in 2007, according to its "Costly Reactions" report. For growing north-west suburbs like Dean Park, that trend makes dedicated egg-free bakeries more relevant every year, not less.

At Num Num's, eggs are never used in any product, preparation area or piece of equipment on-site. The whole kitchen is egg-free, not just one product line. That distinction matters for several groups across Dean Park and the surrounding suburbs:

  • Hindu vegetarian and Jain households — many Hindu and Jain dietary traditions exclude eggs; our full menu qualifies on the egg axis.
  • Sikh families — some Sikh dietary codes exclude eggs, and Num Num's is fully compliant on that measure.
  • Halal-conscious households — egg-free baking removes one common allergen concern, though customers should confirm any additional halal-specific requirements directly with us.
  • Vegan or plant-based customers — note our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter); we are egg-free, not vegan.
  • Egg allergy sufferers — for severe or anaphylactic allergies, always state your needs clearly when ordering. Follow your own medical or ASCIA-aligned advice; we don't promise medical-grade safety beyond a fully egg-free kitchen.
