What Makes Num Num's Cakes 100% Eggless?

A cake is genuinely "100% eggless" only when eggs are absent from every recipe, every surface, and every piece of equipment — not just left out of one order. Egg allergy affects roughly 9% of Australian infants, according to Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia, and for those families cross-contamination from a kitchen that uses eggs elsewhere is a real hazard. An entirely egg-free kitchen removes that pathway by design.

Here's what most people don't realise: getting an eggless cake right is genuinely hard, which is why so many general bakeries produce a flat, dense version. Eggs do three jobs at once — binding, leavening, and adding moisture — and replacing all three without a heavy, gummy crumb takes years of recipe work across every flavour. That single-minded focus is the whole reason a dedicated eggless bakery exists, and it's the difference Winston Hills customers notice the moment they cut the first slice.
