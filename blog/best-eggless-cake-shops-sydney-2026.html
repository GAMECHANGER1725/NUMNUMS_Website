10 Best Eggless Cake Shops in Sydney 2026

5 June 2026 12 min read Sydney Bakeries

Finding a genuinely eggless cake in Sydney is harder than it sounds. Search for "eggless cake near me" and you'll get a mix of bakeries that are truly egg-free, others that offer a few eggless options alongside their regular egg-based menu, and some that label vegan cakes as eggless without distinguishing between the two. That distinction matters — especially if you're buying for someone with an egg allergy, a South Asian vegetarian household, or a Jain family celebration.

This is an honest, independently researched list of 10 Sydney bakeries that offer eggless cakes. We're transparent: Num Num's Bakery is our business, and we're ranked first. But every other bakery listed here is real, and the information about them is drawn from their own public websites and menus. We've clearly noted which operations are 100% eggless and which offer eggless as a partial option — because that difference is genuinely important for many customers.

Key Takeaways
  • Only 6 of the 10 bakeries listed are fully eggless operations — the others offer eggless as one option among many.
  • For egg allergy customers, a 100% eggless kitchen matters — partial bakeries may have cross-contamination risk.
  • Several "eggless" bakeries are actually vegan — suitable for vegans but not the same product as a dairy-inclusive eggless cake.
  • Indian-style eggless flavours (Rasmalai, Kesar Pista, Gulab Jamun) are available at Num Num's and EatWithSimer.

How We Ranked This List

Our ranking is based on four criteria: (1) whether the bakery is fully eggless or offers it as a partial option; (2) the range and quality of eggless cake offerings; (3) Sydney-wide service coverage; (4) suitability for South Asian dietary requirements — which is the core need driving most "eggless cake Sydney" searches. We've been transparent about our own place on the list and our connection to Num Num's Bakery, so you can factor that in however you like.

Note on the next two bakeries

Miss Sina and Oh My Days are 100% vegan bakeries — they produce no eggs or dairy. Technically all their products are eggless, but they are a different product category: vegan cakes use plant-based fats and milks instead of butter and cream. If you need fully dairy-free as well as egg-free, they are excellent options.

Which Bakery Is Right for You?

If you have an egg allergy (or are buying for someone who does)

Go with a 100% eggless operation — Num Num's, Bake n' Flake, Bakealicious By Gabriela, EatWithSimer, or Nam's Eggless Bakes. A kitchen that also bakes egg-based products introduces cross-contamination risk. For severe allergies, always contact the bakery directly and confirm their protocols regardless of what a website states.

If you're buying for a South Asian vegetarian or Jain household

Eggless (with dairy) is the correct category — not vegan. Num Num's Bakery, EatWithSimer, and Bake n' Flake are the best-matched options given their Indian flavour profiles. Num Num's is the only one combining custom cakes with a full traditional Indian sweets range under the same 100% eggless roof.

If you need fully dairy-free as well as egg-free (vegan)

Miss Sina and Oh My Days are both genuinely excellent. They are dedicated vegan operations with award-winning products. Neither will match the flavour profile of a dairy-inclusive eggless cake, but for fully vegan guests they are the correct choice in Sydney.

If you need a luxury custom cake and budget is flexible

Blow The Candle at the high end for large tiered designs. Num Num's Bakery, Bakealicious By Gabriela, and Nam's Eggless Bakes for premium custom work within the 100% eggless category. Zest Patisserie for Hills District customers who want a patisserie finish.

If you are in north-west Sydney (Marsden Park, Schofields, Riverstone, Quakers Hill)

Num Num's Bakery has a dedicated store at Riverstone Shopping Centre. Bake n' Flake in Parklea covers the Quakers Hill and Blacktown corridor. Both are 100% eggless. See the full guide to eggless cakes in Riverstone.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best 100% eggless cake shop in Sydney?

Num Num's Bakery in Harris Park and Riverstone is Sydney's only dedicated 100% eggless bakery combining custom cakes with Indian sweets. Every product is made without eggs — no cross-contamination risk from egg-based baking. Disclosure: Num Num's Bakery is this website's owner.

Is there a difference between a 100% eggless bakery and one that offers eggless options?

Yes — and it matters significantly for allergy customers. A 100% eggless bakery has no eggs on the premises. A bakery offering eggless as an option alongside conventional products may share ovens, mixing bowls, and surfaces with egg-containing products. If egg allergy is a concern, always ask directly about cross-contamination protocols.

Which Sydney bakeries make eggless Indian-style cakes?

Num Num's Bakery offers the broadest Indian eggless range: Rasmalai, Kesar Pista, Gulab Jamun, and more — plus traditional Indian sweets. EatWithSimer in Eastwood also specialises in Indian fusion eggless cakes. Bake n' Flake in Parklea incorporates Indian pastries alongside cakes.

Are eggless cakes and vegan cakes the same thing?

No. Eggless cakes remove eggs but still contain dairy — butter, milk, yoghurt, cream. Vegan cakes remove all animal products including dairy. All vegan cakes are eggless, but most eggless cakes are not vegan. For South Asian vegetarian and Jain households, eggless-with-dairy is the traditional and correct choice. Read the full comparison.

Can I order an eggless cake online for same-day delivery in Sydney?

Most specialist eggless bakeries require 48 hours' notice for custom cakes — these are made fresh to order, not mass-produced. Num Num's Bakery, Bakealicious By Gabriela, and Zest Patisserie offer next-day delivery in Sydney for certain products. For same-day, your best option is visiting a physical shopfront directly.
