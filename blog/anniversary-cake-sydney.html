Eggless Anniversary Cakes in Sydney: From the 1st to the Golden 50th

By Tarun Patel 4 June 2026 9 min read Custom Cakes · Anniversary

An anniversary cake carries more weight than a regular birthday cake. It marks a partnership, a milestone, a year — or fifty — of showing up for each other. So the cake should feel like an occasion, and it should be one that every guest at the table can actually eat. That's where a 100% eggless cake earns its place: in a city as diverse as Sydney, the right anniversary cake includes everyone.

At Num Num's Bakery, every cake is eggless as standard — not a substitution, the whole menu — across 15 flavours and any custom design you can picture. Whether it's a quiet first anniversary for two or a golden 50th for a hall full of family, we make it fresh to order from two Sydney shops. Here's how to choose, size and order the right one.

What Makes a Great Anniversary Cake?

A great anniversary cake balances three things: a flavour the couple loves, a design that suits the milestone, and a size that feeds the room without waste. Unlike a children's party cake, an anniversary cake usually leans elegant — clean fondant, fresh florals, gold or silver accents, perhaps the years written in script. The decoration should feel grown-up, even when the celebration is joyful and loud.

The fourth ingredient, and the one couples increasingly ask for first, is inclusivity. With about 38% of Greater Sydney residents born overseas (ABS, 2021 Census), most anniversary guest lists span cultures and diets. An eggless cake quietly solves that — one centrepiece everyone can share, no separate plate for the relatives who don't eat egg.

For an anniversary with a mixed guest list, a 100% eggless cake is the single most inclusive choice — it suits egg allergy, Hindu vegetarian and Jain diets without a second cake.

Which Cake Flavours Suit an Anniversary?

From our internal order data, anniversary cakes skew towards the richer, more grown-up flavours. Ferrero Rocher, Tiramisu, Red Velvet and Chocolate top the list, with Rasmalai a firm favourite for South Asian couples marking a milestone. These read as celebration flavours — indulgent without being childish.

That said, the right flavour is the one the couple actually loves — a first-anniversary cake echoing the wedding flavour is a lovely touch. The whole 15-flavour menu is on the table, every one eggless. Browse them on the Our Cakes page, where there's no separate egg-free section because the entire range qualifies.

What Are the Milestone Anniversaries to Celebrate?

Every anniversary is worth marking, but the milestones invite a bigger cake and a themed design. The traditional symbols give a ready-made colour palette — silver for the 25th, ruby red for the 40th, gold for the 50th — which translates beautifully onto fondant and piping.

  • 1st (paper) — often an echo of the wedding cake; intimate, two-person size, the wedding flavour revisited.
  • 5th & 10th — a growing family celebration; a 10-inch cake with a simple elegant finish.
  • 25th (silver) — silver and white styling, often two-tier for a larger guest list.
  • 40th (ruby) — deep red roses, burgundy accents, a Red Velvet or Chocolate base.
  • 50th (golden) — gold leaf, "50" toppers, a statement two-tier centrepiece for an extended family.

You don't have to follow the traditional symbol, of course — plenty of couples simply pick their favourite colours or the palette of their wedding day. The themes are a starting point, not a rule. What they do well is give a designer a clear direction, so a quick "ruby red, gold script, Chocolate base for our 40th" is often all we need to sketch something that feels properly considered rather than generic.

Should You Order One Cake or a Dessert Table?

For most anniversaries a single statement cake is plenty — it gives you the moment, the photo and the cut. But for a larger milestone, pairing a smaller showpiece cake with a spread of sweets often works better than one enormous tier. The cake stays the centrepiece for the speeches, while the dessert table keeps a long guest list grazing happily through the evening.

This is where being a South Asian bakery helps. Alongside cakes, we make Indian sweets — mithai that sits naturally on a celebration table next to a Rasmalai or Ferrero Rocher cake. A 50th with a hundred guests might run a two-tier cake for the cut and a tray of sweets and cupcakes for the rest of the room. If you're weighing it up, message us your headcount and budget, and we'll suggest the split that gives the best presence without over-ordering.

The same inclusivity rule applies across the whole table: every item we make is egg-free, so nobody has to check a label or skip the dessert. One kitchen, one standard, no exceptions — whether it's the headline cake or the last cupcake.

Why Choose an Eggless Anniversary Cake?

You choose an eggless anniversary cake because it includes everyone at the celebration, and because demand for egg-free options has never been higher. As of August 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence reported 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — now live with allergic disease, double the 2007 figure. On a 50-person guest list, that's a real number of people for whom egg-free isn't a preference but a need.

Beyond allergy, many Sydney families keep vegetarian kitchens where eggs are excluded, especially in Hindu and Jain households. An eggless cake means the grandparents who don't eat egg, the cousin with an allergy, and the kids all eat the same celebration cake. One cake, no exceptions, no awkward conversations — which is exactly what an anniversary should feel like.

Here's something couples tell us again and again: they assumed an eggless cake would be the compromise option, and were surprised it was simply the better one. Nobody at the party can tell the difference in taste, and nobody has to be left out. The "special diet" cake and the "real" cake become the same cake — and that, quietly, is the whole point of a celebration.

How Much Cake Do You Need for an Anniversary Party?

Match the cake size to your guest count. As a rough guide, an 8-inch round serves about 20, a 10-inch about 35, a 12-inch about 50, and a two-tier cake comfortably feeds 70 or more. For a milestone with speeches and a big extended family, size up — a cake that runs out before the back table is served is the one regret people remember.

Not sure where you land? Tell us your guest count on WhatsApp and we'll recommend a size, or split it across two flavours so there's something for everyone. The Order Online page has a fuller sizing guide too.

What Are Some Anniversary Cake Design Ideas?

A design doesn't need to be complicated to feel special. From the custom orders we make most, these ideas suit an anniversary beautifully:

  • Number cakes — a sculpted "25" or "50" in the couple's colours, a modern centrepiece for a milestone year. See our number cakes guide for sizing.
  • Photo-print topper — an edible print of the wedding photo or the couple today, a sentimental finishing touch.
  • Wedding-flavour echo — recreate the original wedding cake flavour for a first or tenth anniversary.
  • Two-tier elegance — fresh florals, fondant draping and a script topper for a formal celebration.
  • Metallic theme — silver for the 25th, gold for the 50th, ruby red for the 40th, drawn from the traditional anniversary palette.

Whatever the design, the foundation never changes: 100% eggless, made fresh, with no shortcut on flavour. For more inspiration, see our celebration cakes and custom cake guides.

A Note on Ingredients and Allergens

To be clear about what's in the cake: our kitchen is 100% egg-free, which removes eggs entirely. But our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter, and fresh cream on some designs), some involve nuts, and standard sponges contain wheat. We're an eggless bakery — not a vegan, nut-free or gluten-free one.

If a guest manages a serious allergy beyond eggs, tell us when you order on WhatsApp and follow your own medical advice. We'll provide a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour so you can plan the celebration with confidence.
