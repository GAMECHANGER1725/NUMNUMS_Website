Why Is an Eggless New Year Cake Important for Sydney Families?

In 2025, 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — live with allergic disease, according to the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report (August 2025). That figure has doubled from 4.1 million (19.6%) in 2007. For celebration events like New Year — where the cake is cut and shared among everyone at the table — a cake that excludes egg allergy sufferers or violates religious dietary practice is a real planning problem. Having one eggless option on a dessert table doesn't solve it. Having an entire menu of eggless cakes does.

Sydney's cultural diversity makes this especially relevant at New Year. Many Hindu vegetarian households observe lacto-vegetarian practice that excludes eggs year-round — including at New Year celebrations. Muslim families may have concerns about cross-contamination in bakeries that handle non-halal products. And for families with younger children who have documented egg allergy, a shared-kitchen risk is not something they can dismiss. At Num Num's, the entire bakery is egg-free — from every recipe to every preparation surface and piece of equipment.

In August 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence found 30% of Australians — 8.2 million people — now live with allergic disease, up from 19.6% (4.1 million) in 2007 (NACE, Costly Reactions report, August 2025). At a New Year's Eve party of 30 guests, statistically around 9 will have some form of allergic disease. A dedicated eggless cake ensures everyone is included.

Are Eggless Cakes Suitable for All New Year Celebrations in Sydney?

Sydney celebrates multiple New Year occasions across the calendar, and Num Num's eggless cakes are appropriate for all of them. According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), eggs are one of Australia's eight priority food allergens subject to mandatory labelling — a classification that underscores how common and significant egg sensitivity is across the population.

The occasions our customers celebrate with eggless New Year cakes include:

  • Gregorian New Year (31 December / 1 January) — the biggest order window; Ferrero Rocher and Tiramisu dominate
  • Chinese New Year (January or February) — Lychee, Mango, and Vanilla are popular choices for this celebration

  • Hindu New Year (Diwali, Ugadi, Gudi Padwa — varies) — Rasmalai, Mango, and Vanilla are the consistent choices
