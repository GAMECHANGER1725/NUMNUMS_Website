Why Is Eggless Cake So Hard to Find Near Newington?

In 2025, 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — lived with allergic disease, according to the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report, up from just 4.1 million (19.6%) in 2007. Egg allergy specifically affects roughly 9% of Australian infants, per Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia. Despite that, most bakeries around Newington and Sydney Olympic Park still treat egg-free baking as an occasional substitution rather than a core offering.

The reason is straightforward: reformulating a cake recipe without eggs from scratch is genuinely difficult. Egg proteins bind, aerate and add moisture — remove them without proper technique and you get a dense, dry, flat result. Building a menu around egg-free baking from day one takes years of recipe development. Most general bakeries won't make that investment for what they see as a niche request. It's the only thing Num Num's Bakery does.

Why Do Newington Families Need a 100% Eggless Bakery?

Eggs are one of Australia's eight priority food allergens under Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) labelling rules. Cross-contamination risk in a shared kitchen is a genuine concern for egg-allergic families. At Num Num's Bakery, eggs are never brought into the building at all — not in any recipe, any prep area, or any piece of equipment. That removes the cross-contamination risk entirely, rather than just managing it with careful labelling.

This distinction matters for more than allergy sufferers. It also matters for:

  • Hindu vegetarian households — many Hindu dietary traditions treat eggs as non-vegetarian, so an entirely egg-free menu removes the guesswork.
  • Sikh families — some Sikh dietary codes exclude eggs, and Sydney's western suburbs have significant Sikh communities.
  • Halal-observant households — a fully egg-free kitchen sidesteps a category of cross-contamination questions that mixed bakeries can't always answer confidently.
  • Jain households — Jain dietary practice excludes eggs; the entire Num Num's menu is Jain-compatible on that measure.
  • Vegan or plant-based customers — note that our cakes still contain dairy (milk powder, butter); we're an egg-free bakery, not a vegan one.

Have allergen questions beyond eggs? For a severe or anaphylactic allergy in particular, contact us directly via WhatsApp before ordering. Follow your own medical or ASCIA-aligned advice too. We'll walk you through a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour, but we don't promise medical-grade safety beyond the egg-free guarantee.
