Why Order an Eggless Cake Rather Than a Regular One?

In 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence found that 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — now live with allergic disease, double the 4.1 million (19.6%) recorded in 2007 (NACE, Costly Reactions report, August 2025). Egg is one of the most common childhood allergens, affecting roughly 9% of Australian infants (Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia). Order an eggless cake and you've quietly covered a guest list you can't fully predict.

There's a cultural reason too. Many Hindu vegetarian, Jain, and other households exclude eggs entirely, so an eggless cake is simply the most inclusive option for a mixed gathering — one dessert everyone can share. And done properly, eggless doesn't mean a compromise on taste or texture; a cake built without eggs from the start can be every bit as soft and moist as a conventional one.

How Is an Eggless Cake Made Without Eggs?

From our years of baking nothing but eggless cakes, the honest answer is that you don't simply remove the eggs — you rebuild the recipe around their absence. Eggs do three jobs in a conventional cake: they bind the batter, help it rise, and add moisture. Take them out of an ordinary recipe and it falls flat and dry. A good eggless cake replaces each of those jobs deliberately, which is why it tastes nothing like a cake with the eggs simply left out.

The binding and structure come from a careful balance of flour, leavening, and ingredients like milk and yoghurt, while the rise is managed with the right ratio of baking powder and bicarb. Moisture — the thing most failed eggless cakes get wrong — comes from fats and dairy worked into the batter so the crumb stays soft for days, not hours. It's chemistry as much as craft, and it only works when the whole kitchen is set up for it.

An eggless cake is not a regular cake with the eggs removed; it is a recipe rebuilt to replace the three jobs eggs do — binding, rising, and moisture — using a balance of flour, leavening, fats and dairy. This is why a dedicated eggless bakery produces a softer, more consistent crumb than a one-off substitution at a general bakery.

That's the whole reason we chose to be a dedicated eggless bakery rather than one that offers an eggless option on the side. When every cake follows the same egg-free method, you get consistency — the cake you order online turns out the same every time. What Size Eggless Cake Should You Order Online?

From our experience, sizing is where most online orders need a quick nudge — people picture a cake but not a portion count. As a rule, plan for one slice per guest plus a few spare, then round up. A cake that's slightly too big becomes happy leftovers; one that's too small leaves people sharing.

  • 6-inch round — 8–10 portions, small family gathering
  • 8-inch round — 15–20 portions, the most-ordered size
  • 10-inch round — 30–40 portions, larger parties
  • Two-tier — 40-plus, milestone events and statement cakes

If you tell us the guest count when you message, we'll recommend a size — that's part of what makes ordering through WhatsApp easier than a fixed online checkout. The full guide also lives on the Order Online page if you prefer to work it out first.

Can You Order an Eggless Cake Online for Allergies and Diets?

Yes — with one honest caveat. Every Num Num's cake is made in a 100% egg-free kitchen, which removes the egg cross-contamination pathway and suits Hindu vegetarian, Jain, and other egg-avoiding diets. Egg is one of the priority allergens that must be declared on Australian food labels (Food Standards Australia New Zealand), and an egg-free kitchen takes that risk off the table.

What an eggless cake is not is allergen-free across the board. Our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter), may involve nuts, and standard cakes contain wheat. So an eggless cake is a real solution for an egg allergy or an egg-free diet — but it isn't automatically safe for a dairy, nut, or gluten allergy.

For a severe or anaphylactic egg allergy, tell us when you order and follow your own medical and ASCIA-aligned advice. For any allergy beyond eggs, message us first and we'll give you a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour so you can order with confidence.
