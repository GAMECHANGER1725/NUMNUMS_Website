Are Eggless Cakes Suitable for Dietary and Cultural Needs in Dundas?

Egg-free baking aligns with several dietary frameworks represented across the City of Parramatta LGA that includes Dundas. According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ), eggs are one of the eight priority food allergens in Australia, with strict labelling laws in place. For egg-allergic families, cross-contamination from a bakery that uses eggs in other products remains a genuine risk.

At Num Num's Bakery, eggs are not used in any product, any preparation area, or any equipment on-site. That's an important distinction: it's not just an ingredient swap, it's the entire kitchen environment. This matters for:

  • Hindu vegetarian households — many Hindu dietary traditions exclude eggs as a form of lacto-vegetarian practice
  • Sikh families — some Sikh dietary codes exclude eggs; a portion of our Dundas and Parramatta-area customers order on this basis
  • Halal-observant households — eggs from conventional sources are considered halal, but cross-contamination with non-halal ingredients in a mixed bakery can be a concern. Our entirely egg-free kitchen sidesteps this category of question entirely.
  • Jain households — Jain dietary practice excludes eggs; our entire menu is Jain-compatible on the egg axis
  • Vegan or plant-based families — note that our cakes contain dairy (milk powder, butter); we're not a vegan bakery, but we are fully egg-free

If you have specific allergen questions beyond eggs, contact us directly via WhatsApp before ordering — standard cakes contain wheat and may involve nuts in some flavours, and we'll give you a complete ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour. For anyone managing a severe or anaphylactic egg allergy, we recommend also following your own medical advice; our egg-free kitchen removes the egg cross-contamination pathway, but it isn't a substitute for guidance from bodies like Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia.
