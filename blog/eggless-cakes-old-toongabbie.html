Why Is Eggless Cake So Hard to Find Near Old Toongabbie?

In 2025, egg allergy affects approximately 9% of Australian infants, according to Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia. But the demand for egg-free baking extends well beyond childhood allergy. As of August 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report (produced with Deloitte Access Economics) found that 8.2 million Australians, or 30% of the population, now live with allergic disease. In 2007, that figure was 4.1 million (19.6%). Western Sydney's diverse communities add religious and cultural dimensions on top of this medical picture.

The reason dedicated eggless bakeries remain rare has everything to do with technical complexity. Removing eggs from a cake recipe means simultaneously replacing structure (eggs set when baked, creating the sponge's frame), moisture retention (egg yolks hold water in the crumb), leavening assistance (egg whites trap air), and emulsification (keeping fat and water unified in the batter). No single substitute does all four jobs equally well across every flavour profile. Developing reliable eggless recipes for 15 distinct flavours requires years of formulation work, an investment most generalist bakeries don't make because their egg-free volume doesn't justify it.
