What Is Guru Nanak Gurpurab and When Does It Fall in 2026?

Guru Nanak Gurpurab falls on Wednesday, 5 November 2026. It marks the birth anniversary of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the first Sikh Guru, born in 1469. "Gurpurab" means "Guru's remembrance day." The date follows Kartik Purnima, the full moon of the Hindu lunar month of Kartik. The Gregorian calendar date shifts each year, usually landing somewhere in late October or November.

The day is also called Guru Nanak Jayanti or Prakash Utsav. Celebrations begin two days early with an Akhand Path: a continuous, unbroken 48-hour reading of the Guru Granth Sahib, the Sikh holy scripture. Readers rotate in shifts so the recitation never stops. A day before the main date, many gurdwaras hold a Nagar Kirtan, a street procession led by the Panj Pyare carrying the Nishan Sahib. The main day itself opens with prayer at dawn and closes with a shared meal.

How Large Is Sydney's Sikh Community, and Where Is It Growing?

Sikhism is Australia's fastest-growing major religion. The 2021 ABS Census counted 210,400 Sikhs nationally, up from roughly 72,000 in 2011. That's almost a threefold increase in one decade. Western Sydney holds a large share of that growth, and it sits close to both of our shops.

Riverstone itself shows the same trend at the local level. Punjabi was spoken at home by 2.8% of Riverstone residents at the 2021 ABS Census QuickStats for Riverstone, more than double the 1.2% recorded in 2016. Blacktown LGA, which surrounds our Riverstone shop, sits at roughly 4.6% Sikh according to the Blacktown City community profile — well above the Greater Sydney average of 0.7%.

Why Do Many Sikh Families Choose an Eggless Cake for Gurpurab?

Many Sikh families choose an eggless cake because a large share of Sikh households follow a lacto-vegetarian diet and avoid eggs for religious reasons. That makes a standard bakery cake unsuitable without substitution. A cake that's egg-free by default removes that question entirely, so it can sit on the table without anyone needing to check an ingredient list first.

This isn't a fringe preference. For many Punjabi and Sikh households, vegetarian eating is simply the norm, passed down through generations and tied to religious observance. A cake containing eggs is something that has to be avoided outright, not just noted. That's a real, practical reason a dedicated eggless bakery matters for this community — not a marketing angle.

What Happens at the Gurdwara on Gurpurab Day?

Gurdwaras mark Gurpurab with a set sequence built around scripture, procession and shared food. The Akhand Path begins two days before the main date. It's a continuous 48-hour reading of the Guru Granth Sahib, with volunteer readers rotating in shifts so the recitation never breaks. The unbroken reading symbolises that the Guru's word is always present.

The day before Gurpurab, many gurdwaras hold a Nagar Kirtan. This is a colourful procession through local streets. It's led by the Panj Pyare — five Sikhs dressed in saffron robes — carrying the Nishan Sahib flag and the Guru Granth Sahib in a decorated palanquin. On the main day itself, prayers start early, followed by kirtan and katha. The day concludes with langar: the free community kitchen Guru Nanak founded to serve everyone equally, regardless of caste, faith or background.

Langar is the heart of the day, and it's worth being precise about what a cake is and isn't in this context. It's a small, modern addition families bring home after the gurdwara service — never a replacement for the langar meal or the traditional mithai already on the table. Are Eggless Cakes Soft Enough for a Large Community Gathering?

Yes — a well-made eggless cake is soft, moist and holds its shape under decoration, and most guests can't tell it was baked without eggs. The dry, dense reputation eggless cake sometimes carries comes from bakeries that simply remove the egg from a standard recipe and leave the gap unfilled, rather than reformulating properly.

Our recipes are built from scratch to be eggless, using ingredients that replace what eggs do for binding, lift and moisture. That matters for a Gurpurab cake serving a large, mixed-age crowd across an afternoon of prayer, food and conversation. One honest note for transparency: our cakes are fully eggless but not vegan, since they contain dairy such as milk powder and butter, and standard cakes contain wheat. If anyone in your group has allergy questions beyond eggs, message us before ordering for a full ingredient breakdown.
