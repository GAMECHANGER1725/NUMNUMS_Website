Are Eggless Cakes Safe for Egg Allergies and Cultural Diets Near Plumpton?

Egg-free baking removes the egg cross-contamination pathway, and eggs are one of the eight priority food allergens recognised under Australian law, according to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ). At Num Num's Bakery, eggs are never brought into any product, preparation area, or piece of equipment on-site — that removes the single biggest cross-contamination risk for egg-allergic families.

It's important to be upfront about what "egg-free" doesn't cover. Our cakes still contain dairy — milk powder and butter feature across the range — and standard sponge recipes contain wheat. We are not a nut-free or gluten-free kitchen. For families managing a severe or anaphylactic egg allergy, tell us clearly when ordering and continue to follow your own doctor's or ASCIA-aligned advice — we'll give you a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour, but we can't promise medical-grade safety beyond the egg-free kitchen itself.

That egg-free standard also aligns naturally with several dietary frameworks common across Plumpton and the wider Blacktown LGA:

  • Hindu vegetarian households — many Hindu dietary traditions treat eggs as non-vegetarian (lacto-vegetarian practice)
  • Sikh families — some Sikh dietary codes exclude eggs entirely
  • Jain households — Jain dietary practice excludes eggs; our entire menu is Jain-compatible on the egg axis
  • Halal-observant households — cross-contamination with non-halal meats in a mixed bakery can be a concern; our entirely egg-free kitchen sidesteps this category of question
  • Vegan or plant-based families — note our cakes contain dairy; we're not a vegan bakery, but we are fully egg-free

If you have specific allergen questions beyond eggs, message us directly on WhatsApp before ordering and we'll walk through the full ingredient list for your chosen flavour.
