Eggless Cake Tasting Sydney: Sample Before You Commit

By Tarun Patel 14 July 2026 11 min read Custom Cakes · Eggless Cakes

Ordering a large custom cake sight unseen is a gamble most people don't think about until the day it goes wrong. A three-tier wedding cake, a 60-guest birthday order, or a corporate celebration cake is expensive to get wrong and impossible to remake overnight. That's exactly why an eggless cake tasting exists — a short appointment where you sample real slices before you sign off on flavour, size, and design for the big day.

At Num Num's Bakery, every cake across both our Harris Park and Riverstone locations is 100% eggless, and eggless baking behaves differently to a standard egg-based sponge. The crumb, the rise, and the moisture all come from a different formulation, so tasting matters even more here than at a conventional bakery. This guide covers how our tastings work, how many flavours to sample, what a tasting box costs, and — most importantly — how far ahead of your event to book one.

What Is a Cake Tasting, and Why Should You Book One Before a Big Order?

A cake tasting is a short, arranged sampling session where you try real portions of a bakery's flavours before committing to a full-size order. From our order records across Harris Park and Riverstone, tasting requests are concentrated almost entirely among tiered wedding cakes, milestone birthday orders above 3kg, and corporate cakes feeding 50-plus guests — the orders where a wrong flavour choice is the most costly to fix.

The logic is simple: photos and menu descriptions can't tell you whether a Ferrero Rocher sponge is dense or light, or whether our Rasmalai flavour reads as too subtle for a big crowd. Only a fork test does that. It also gives us a chance to talk through size and tier options face to face, rather than guessing over text.

How Do Eggless Cake Tastings Work at Num Num's Bakery?

From our WhatsApp order history, most tasting requests follow the same pattern: a customer messages +61 425 697 725, nominates 3 to 5 flavours from the 15 on offer, and we confirm a pick-up slot for a small tasting box at either Harris Park or Riverstone. There's no formal sit-down appointment required — it's built around collection, not a table booking.

Nationally, formal wedding cake tastings at Australian bakeries typically cost between $20 and $100, reflecting the time it takes to bake, portion, and present each flavour properly. We keep our process lighter-weight: a tasting box of small slices you can share at home, at a pace that suits you, rather than a rushed 20-minute appointment squeezed between other bookings.

Once you've settled on a flavour, we move straight into the order conversation — size, filling, buttercream or fondant finish, and your pick-up date. The tasting result carries directly into that order; there's no need to re-explain your preferences from scratch.

How Many Flavours Should You Sample Before Choosing?

From our internal order data, most customers who book a tasting sample exactly three flavours: one familiar option (Chocolate or Vanilla), one fruit-forward option (Mango, Strawberry, or Lychee), and one premium option (Ferrero Rocher or Tiramisu). That spread covers the range of tastes usually represented across a mixed guest list.

Sampling more than five flavours in one sitting tends to blur the palate rather than sharpen the decision — professional wine and cheese tastings hit the same wall, which is why most formal tasting menus cap out around four or five items. If you're genuinely torn between two flavours, ask us about a half-and-half tier instead of trying to sample your way to certainty.

Why Does Tasting Matter More for Eggless Cakes Than Regular Cakes?

In 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report found that 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — now live with allergic disease, up from 4.1 million (19.6%) in 2007. Food allergy alone affects around 7% of the population, and per Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia, egg allergy affects approximately 9% of Australian infants. Demand for genuinely egg-free baking has never been higher.

Eggless baking replaces eggs with a different combination of binders, acids, and leavening agents. Done well, it produces a soft, moist crumb that's genuinely indistinguishable from an egg-based sponge. Done poorly, it can taste dense, dry, or gummy — and unlike a regular bakery where "eggless" is a rare special request, at Num Num's it's the entire menu, refined over years specifically to close that gap.

How Far Ahead Should You Book a Tasting Before a Wedding or Big Event?

From our order calendar across both locations, wedding and large milestone cake tastings should be booked 8 to 12 weeks before the event date, giving enough runway to lock in a flavour, revisit the design brief, and still meet the standard 48-hour order notice comfortably. Smaller celebrations need far less lead time.

Weddings & large milestone events — 8-12 weeks ahead
Tiered cakes, elaborate fondant work, and 50-plus-guest orders benefit from the longest runway. This also gives time for a second tasting if your first choice doesn't land.
Birthdays & smaller milestone celebrations — 3-4 weeks ahead
Enough time to sample two or three flavours and still confirm the full order with plenty of buffer before the 48-hour minimum notice window.
Corporate & casual bookings — 1-2 weeks ahead
A quick tasting box is usually enough to confirm a safe, crowd-pleasing flavour for an office or community event.
Festival periods (Diwali, Eid, Christmas, school holidays)
Add one to two extra weeks on top of the above windows — tasting and order slots fill fastest during these periods.

What Questions Should You Ask During a Tasting Consultation?

The most useful tasting questions go beyond "which flavour tastes best" — from our experience running consultations, the customers who leave happiest ask about how the cake will hold up on the day, not just how it tastes in the box. Here's the shortlist we recommend working through.

  1. How will this flavour taste after 4-6 hours at room temperature? Fillings and buttercream behave differently once a cake has been sitting out at a reception or party.
  2. Can this flavour support a fondant finish, or is it better suited to buttercream? Denser flavours like Ferrero Rocher generally hold fondant tiers better than lighter fruit sponges.
  3. How does the portion size scale for my guest count? Ask us to translate your tasting slice into the right cake size via the Order Online page's sizing guide.
  4. What's the full ingredient list? Especially relevant if any guests have allergies beyond eggs — our kitchen is entirely egg-free but does use dairy, and standard sponges contain wheat.
  5. What's the realistic order timeline from tasting to pick-up? Confirm your 48-hour (or longer, for tiered designs) notice window before you leave the tasting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to book a cake tasting before ordering a custom cake?

It isn't compulsory, but it's strongly recommended for tiered, wedding, or large celebration cakes above 3kg. From our order records, tasting requests are concentrated almost entirely among multi-tier and 50-plus-guest orders. Message +61 425 697 725 on WhatsApp to arrange one.

How much does a cake tasting cost at Num Num's Bakery?

Tasting arrangements are discussed directly on WhatsApp and vary with the number of flavours and portion size requested. Nationally, formal wedding cake tastings typically cost $20 to $100 at Australian bakeries — ask us for current tasting-box pricing before you book.

How far in advance should I book a tasting before my wedding or event?

Book 8 to 12 weeks ahead for weddings and large milestone events, 3 to 4 weeks for birthdays and smaller celebrations, and 1 to 2 weeks for corporate or casual bookings. Add extra time around Diwali, Eid, and Christmas, when slots fill up fastest.

Can I taste multiple eggless flavours in one sitting?

Yes. Most customers sample 3 to 5 flavours from our 15-flavour menu in one tasting box, typically one classic option, one fruit flavour, and one premium flavour like Ferrero Rocher or Tiramisu. See the full Our Cakes page for the complete list.

Do I still need to give 48 hours notice after a tasting?

Yes — the tasting confirms your flavour choice, but the full cake order still needs a minimum of 48 hours notice, and 4 to 5 days for elaborately tiered or heavily decorated designs. Book your tasting well before that window opens.
