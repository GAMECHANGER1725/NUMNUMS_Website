A cake that arrives at someone's door and sits uneaten on the bench because half the family avoids eggs by religion, allergy, or preference isn't a gift — it's an awkward reminder of the thought that went unfinished.

Whether you're gifting for a birthday, a new home, a cultural festival, a corporate milestone, or simply because someone deserves a cake, this guide covers how to choose the right flavour, the right size, and how to order from Sydney's two Num Num's locations.

Why Is a Cake One of Sydney's Best Gift Ideas?

The global gifting market reached approximately $756 billion in 2025, according to Statista's 2025 gifting market report, with food and beverage gifts growing at 6.2% annually — outpacing electronics and clothing. In Sydney specifically, cakes have become the go-to edible gift for a reason: they're celebratory by nature, customisable to the recipient's tastes, and shareable across an entire group. A personalised birthday cake that arrives bearing someone's name carries emotional weight that a gift card never can.

Cakes sit in a unique category among food gifts: they require effort and customisation to feel personal, yet they're immediately understandable as a gesture of celebration and generosity. Unlike a bottle of wine (which excludes many religious communities) or a hamper of charcuterie (which excludes vegetarians), a well-chosen cake is broadly inclusive — especially when the bakery making it has genuinely thought through the dietary needs of Sydney's multicultural population.

Food and beverage gifts have grown at 6.2% annually, faster than electronics (4.1%) or clothing (3.8%) in the same period, according to Statista's 2025 gifting market overview. This growth is driven in part by increasing consumer interest in experiential and perishable gifts — items that are consumed and enjoyed in the moment rather than accumulated. Cakes sit squarely in this category: they create a shared moment, and for the recipient, they communicate something that a physical product rarely does.

For a personal, intimate gift, a mini or single-serve cake communicates thoughtfulness in a different way — smaller, more individual, more like a gesture than a centrepiece.

Are Eggless Gift Cakes Suitable for Everyone in Sydney?

According to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) (foodstandards.gov.au), eggs are a priority allergen in Australia — one of the eight allergens subject to mandatory declaration on food labels. For families managing egg allergy, the concern goes beyond the recipe itself: a bakery that uses eggs in other products introduces cross-contamination risk across shared equipment and surfaces. Num Num's Bakery uses no eggs in any product, preparation area, or equipment on-site — which matters enormously as a baseline assurance for gift-givers who need to be certain.

Beyond allergy, the significance of an eggless cake extends across several dietary frameworks common in Greater Sydney. For Hindu vegetarian households, eggs are excluded under lacto-vegetarian practice. For many Sikh families, egg avoidance is part of dietary observance. For Jain households, eggs are excluded entirely. An eggless cake from a dedicated 100% egg-free bakery is the only gift cake that works without reservation across all of these communities — and it works without needing to announce itself or ask anything of the recipient.

Important allergen note: Num Num's cakes are 100% egg-free but do contain dairy (milk powder, butter) and may involve tree nuts depending on flavour. The bakery is not vegan and not dairy-free. For guests with allergen requirements beyond eggs — including dairy, nuts, or gluten — please contact Num Num's via WhatsApp before ordering and request a full ingredient breakdown for your chosen flavour. For advice on managing severe allergy, the Australasian Society of Clinical Immunology and Allergy (ASCIA) provides comprehensive guidance at allergy.org.au.
