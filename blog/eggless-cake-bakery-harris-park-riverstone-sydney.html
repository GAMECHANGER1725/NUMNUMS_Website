Your Local Eggless Cake Bakery: Harris Park and Riverstone, Western Sydney

6 April 2026 6 min read Locations · Sydney

If you've searched for an eggless cake bakery in western Sydney, the results can be hit and miss. Most bakeries that appear in local search either don't specialise in eggless baking, offer it only as an optional substitution, or operate from home kitchens with limited availability and no consistent pick-up point. Finding a dedicated, properly equipped, every-day-open eggless bakery in the suburbs — one that isn't a cross-town drive — is harder than it should be.

Num Num's Bakery solves that problem with two permanent, staffed locations in western Sydney: Harris Park and Riverstone. Each serves a distinct catchment area, operates with extended hours, and fulfils custom orders made fresh to specification. This guide covers both locations in detail — what's nearby, who they serve, how to order, and what makes each one worth knowing about.

Quick Summary
  • Harris Park: 96/96 Wigram Street — daily 11 am to 10 pm, walk from station
  • Riverstone: Shop 8, Riverstone Shopping Centre — Mon–Fri 6 am–8 pm, Sat–Sun 7 am–7 pm
  • Both 100% eggless — every product on the menu made without eggs
  • 15 flavours, fresh to order — order 48 hours ahead via WhatsApp
  • Serves Parramatta, Westmead, Merrylands, Box Hill, Rouse Hill, Kellyville and beyond

Harris Park: The Original Location

Where It Is and How to Get There

The Harris Park bakery is at 96/96 Wigram Street, Harris Park NSW 2150. It sits in the heart of Harris Park's main strip — the stretch of Wigram and Marion Streets that forms one of Sydney's most vibrant South Asian food and retail precincts. Harris Park train station is a two-minute walk. Parramatta CBD and Westfield Parramatta are roughly a five-minute drive or a short walk along the river.

Parking is available on surrounding streets and in nearby station carparks. For those coming by public transport, Harris Park is on the Main West and Cumberland lines, making it accessible from most of Greater Sydney within 40–60 minutes without a car.

Hours

The Harris Park location is open daily, 11 am to 10 pm — seven days a week, including public holidays. Those late evening hours are particularly useful for anyone picking up a cake after work or coordinating a last-minute addition to a dinner or gathering. Not many bakeries in Sydney maintain 10 pm closing times on weekdays.

The Community It Serves

Harris Park sits inside the broader Parramatta local government area, which is one of the most culturally diverse localities in Australia. The suburb itself has a significant South Asian community — Indian, Sri Lankan, Pakistani, Bangladeshi, and Fijian-Indian families have called this area home for decades, and the Wigram Street precinct reflects that with restaurants, spice shops, sari boutiques, and sweet shops.

For a 100% eggless bakery, this location is exactly right. A large proportion of the community here follows vegetarian or Jain dietary practices where eggs are excluded entirely — not as a health choice, but as a life practice. Num Num's was founded with this community in mind. Harris Park is not just a convenient location; it's where the bakery grew its first loyal customer base.

Suburbs within comfortable distance of Harris Park include: Parramatta, Westmead, Merrylands, Granville, Auburn, Woodville, Guildford, Rosehill, Silverwater, and Homebush.

What to Expect When You Pick Up

Custom orders are placed in advance via WhatsApp and collected at the store. For most custom cakes and cupcake orders, a 48-hour lead time is standard. During peak periods — Diwali, Christmas, long weekends — it's worth ordering 4 to 5 days ahead to guarantee availability. Walk-in availability depends on the day, but ordering ahead is always more reliable for specific flavours or quantities.

Riverstone: The North-Western Location

Where It Is and Who It Serves

The Riverstone store is at Shop 8, Riverstone Shopping Centre, Riverstone NSW 2765. Riverstone sits in Sydney's north-western growth corridor — one of the fastest-expanding residential areas in New South Wales. The suburbs immediately surrounding it include Box Hill, Marsden Park, Schofields, The Ponds, and Rouse Hill, with Kellyville, Stanhope Gardens, Quakers Hill, and Blacktown a short drive further.

This corridor has seen extraordinary population growth since 2015, driven largely by new housing estates and young families. Many of these families have South Asian, Sri Lankan, and South-East Asian backgrounds — communities where eggless dietary requirements are common. The Riverstone location was opened specifically to serve this growing community without requiring a trip to Parramatta.

Hours

Riverstone operates on a schedule that suits the working week: Monday to Friday, 6 am to 8 pm and Saturday to Sunday, 7 am to 7 pm. The early morning opening — 6 am on weekdays — is notable. It means customers can collect orders on the way to work rather than having to plan a separate mid-morning trip.

The Growth Corridor Community

The north-western suburbs of Sydney have a demographic profile that aligns closely with what Num Num's offers. Young families with children, a high proportion of South Asian and Sri Lankan residents, and a strong culture of community celebration — birthdays, festivals, religious occasions, and milestone events — all translate into consistent demand for custom eggless cakes.

The Riverstone Shopping Centre location gives the bakery a stable, permanent presence in a community that previously had no local eggless cake option. Before Num Num's opened here, customers in Box Hill or Marsden Park would need to travel to Parramatta or further into the city for a specialist eggless cake — a 40-minute round trip for something that should be local.

Why Two Locations Matter More Than One

Western Sydney Is Not One Place

From the outside, "western Sydney" looks like a single region. From inside it, it's a collection of distinct communities with their own rhythms, demographics, and needs. Parramatta and Harris Park serve a more established, dense urban community with excellent public transport connections. The north-western corridor is suburban, car-dependent, and expanding rapidly with young families at different life stages.

A bakery with one location in Harris Park serves Parramatta very well and the north-west poorly. One location in Riverstone is the reverse. Two locations — chosen specifically for these two catchments — means that the majority of the western Sydney eggless cake customer base has a pick-up point within 20 minutes of where they live or work.

Same Product, Same Standards at Both

Both locations operate under the same standard: 100% eggless, fresh to order, same 15-flavour range, same WhatsApp ordering process. A customer who moves from Harris Park to Box Hill after buying a house in a new estate doesn't have to find a new bakery. The experience is consistent across both pick-up points.

How to Order from Either Location

  1. Choose your location: Harris Park for the Parramatta area; Riverstone for Box Hill, Schofields, Rouse Hill, and the north-west.
  2. Browse the flavour range: 15 flavours across classic, fruity, premium, and South Asian-inspired categories. See the full range on the Our Cakes page.
  3. Message via WhatsApp: Send your order details — size, flavour, occasion, and preferred pick-up date — to +61 425 697 725. The team will confirm availability and give you a pick-up time.
  4. Allow at least 48 hours: Custom orders are made fresh and need preparation time. For peak periods (Diwali, Christmas, Eid, school holidays), order 4–5 days ahead.
  5. Pick up at the agreed time: Both locations have parking and are easy to access. Your cake will be packed and ready.
