Why Is Eggless Cake So Hard to Find Near Annangrove?

In 2025, 8.2 million Australians — 30% of the population — lived with allergic disease, according to the National Allergy Centre of Excellence's "Costly Reactions" report, up from just 4.1 million (19.6%) in 2007. Egg allergy specifically affects roughly 9% of Australian infants, per Allergy & Anaphylaxis Australia. Despite that scale, most bakeries around Annangrove and Kenthurst still treat egg-free baking as an occasional substitution. Very few treat it as a genuine specialty.

The reason is straightforward. Reformulating a cake recipe without eggs from scratch is genuinely difficult, because egg proteins bind, aerate, and add moisture all at once. Remove them without the right technique and the result is dense, dry, and flat. Building a whole menu around egg-free baking takes years of recipe development. Most general bakeries won't make that investment for what they see as a niche request — it's the only thing Num Num's Bakery does.

In 2025, the National Allergy Centre of Excellence found that 30% of Australians — 8.2 million people — live with allergic disease, roughly double the 2007 figure of 19.6% (NACE, Costly Reactions report, August 2025).
